# Preference learning

Not every reward term is objectively measurable. The canonical problem's [reward composition](../10-physical/01-reward/04-composition.md) includes weights $w_i$ on pressure uniformity, coverage, peak-pressure bounds, and effective-stiffness bounds — and the *right* weighting is not a physical constant. It is a judgment call by the designer about what matters for their application. Preference learning is the sub-system that learns those weights, and learns about parts of the reward that no scalar measurement captures at all (haptic feel, subjective visual match, tactile compliance), from pairwise comparisons and rating data instead of scalar evaluations.

The downstream consumer is [Ch 06's full loop](06-full-loop.md): after each batch of physically-printed designs, the designer rates them in pairs ("A feels more compliant than B") or on a Likert scale; preference learning turns that feedback into a scalar-reward update that the [BayesOpt of Ch 02](02-bayesopt.md) consumes on the next iteration.

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [GP over pairwise ratings](03-preference/00-gp-pairs.md) | Preference GP (Chu & Ghahramani 2005): latent-utility GP with a probit likelihood over pairwise comparisons; posterior inference via Laplace approximation; scales to $n_\text{prefs} \sim 10^3$ |
| [Bradley-Terry model](03-preference/01-bradley-terry.md) | Classical Bradley–Terry (1952) extended with GP priors; tournament-style rating from multiple designers; handles transitivity-violating ratings via likelihood softening |
| [Dueling bandits](03-preference/02-dueling.md) | Online preference acquisition — which pair should the designer rate next? Interleaved filtering (Yue et al. 2012) and KL-based pair selection; cost-aware variant that counts designer-time |

Three claims Ch 03 rests on:

1. **Preference data is the only honest way to learn subjective weights.** The canonical problem's reward has four or five objective terms (each measurable from the simulation: pressure variance, coverage fraction, peak-pressure-barrier value, effective-stiffness-bound distance) and zero or more subjective terms (haptic feel, conformity "feel" on the designer's hand, visual match to intent) that the simulation does *not* produce. A reward-composition weight vector $w$ chosen by fiat embeds the book-author's preferences; a reward-composition weight vector learned from designer ratings embeds the designer's preferences. For a tool that the [Part 1 Ch 03 thesis](../10-physical/03-thesis.md) claims serves real engineering workflows, the second is the only acceptable answer.
2. **Pairwise comparison is cheaper than absolute rating for the same information content.** Eliciting absolute values (rate this design 0–10) is known to produce biased, noisy data — designer calibration drifts, end-of-scale avoidance, order effects. Pairwise comparison (A vs B) gives a noisier raw signal per pair but the signal is unbiased by calibration drift, and the aggregate information-per-designer-minute is higher. `sim-soft` commits to pairwise as the primary modality, with absolute ratings available as a secondary modality for designers who prefer it.
3. **Preference GP composes with BayesOpt GP at the composition layer, not the inference layer.** The reward-composition weights $w$ are part of $\theta$ per [Ch 00's $\theta$ breakdown](00-forward.md); the preference GP's posterior over $w$ multiplies into the objective reward's weight slot, and the BayesOpt GP in [Ch 02](02-bayesopt.md) runs over everything in $\theta$ *other than* $w$. The two GPs don't share kernel machinery or hyperparameters — they are running different inference problems — but they plug into the same forward-map API at the `sim-ml-chassis` boundary. The composition point is the `Surrogate` trait's `call(theta) -> Posterior` method.

The preference sub-system is *not on the Phase A–I critical path* — Phase D's "first working `sim-soft`" uses a hard-coded weight vector for the synthetic 2-parameter demo, and Phase G's live-authoring does the same. The preference infrastructure lands alongside the [physical-print loop from Ch 06](06-full-loop.md), which is explicitly post-Phase-I and not on the Phase A–I roadmap; building it earlier would be building machinery with no source of preference data. Deferred accordingly.
