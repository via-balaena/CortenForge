# The convergence thesis

[§00](00-definitions.md) names four axes the ceiling is measured on. This sub-chapter argues that in 2026 those four axes have converged onto a single architecture — not because the games-branch and science-branch communities reconciled, but because three enabling technical events between 2018 and 2024 removed the historical tradeoff that kept them apart. [Part 1 Ch 03](../../10-physical/03-thesis.md) argues this in engineering detail; the job here is the meta framing that Part 0 owes the reader before the [SOTA survey](../02-sota.md) is readable.

## Why the fork was defensible

From roughly 1995 through 2015 the four axes were on orthogonal tracks. Games physics optimized for real-time on 2005–2015 consumer hardware — the frame budget would not admit implicit Newton on 50 k-tet hyperelastic meshes with IPC barrier updates, so the field adopted [position-based dynamics](../../50-time-integration/01-projective.md), [penalty contact](../../40-contact/00-why-ipc/00-penalty.md), and secondary-motion animation layers to fake viscoelasticity per [Part 2 Ch 07](../../20-materials/07-viscoelastic.md). Science physics optimized for correctness — implicit backward-Euler, [hyperelastic constitutive laws](../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic.md), [IPC-grade non-penetration](../../40-contact/00-why-ipc.md), offline on CPU, ran overnight and called the result valid when the convergence plot said so. Two parallel toolchains — Bullet, PhysX-cloth, XPBD on one side; FEBio, PolyFEM, Abaqus on the other — with near-zero overlap in solver kernels and separate conferences. The fork was not a philosophical disagreement; it was a forced choice under 2015 compute budgets.

## The three enabling events

Between 2018 and 2024 three things changed, each removing one dimension of the tradeoff. [Part 1 Ch 03](../../10-physical/03-thesis.md) argues these three in full; the shapes are:

1. **GPU sparse solvers for FEM matured.** [NVIDIA Warp](../02-sota/01-warp.md) and [Genesis](../02-sota/02-genesis.md) demonstrated that preconditioned conjugate gradient on mesh-derived sparse structure runs at kernel-throughput-bound rates on consumer GPUs. Warp's published neo-Hookean benchmarks reach tens-of-thousands-of-tets scenes at 60+ FPS — well inside the per-character budget of a game engine, comfortably inside the per-scene budget of an engineering design tool. [Part 11 Ch 03 Phase E](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order) derives `sim-soft`'s target envelope from those numbers.
2. **IPC was made practical for real-time.** The original IPC paper (Li et al. 2020, [Part 4 Ch 01](../../40-contact/01-ipc-internals.md)) was offline; the 2022 follow-ups on [adaptive barrier width](../../40-contact/05-real-time/01-barrier-width.md), [GPU-parallel continuous collision detection](../../40-contact/05-real-time/00-gpu.md), and warm-started friction brought the per-step cost into the tens of milliseconds on a mid-tier GPU. The algorithmic components port cleanly to wgpu compute kernels; [Part 8](../../80-gpu/00-wgpu-layout.md) walks the port.
3. **Differentiability came for free.** Once the solver is an energy minimization with smooth total potential $U(x; \theta) = \Psi_\text{elastic} + \Psi_\text{contact} + \Psi_\text{inertia}$, [the adjoint method and the implicit function theorem](../../60-differentiability/02-implicit-function.md) give $\partial x^*/\partial \theta$ at the cost of one back-substitution on the factor already on the tape from the forward solve. This is the foundation of [DiffTaichi](../02-sota/04-difftaichi.md), Genesis, and the differentiable-physics research branch — and it is *only possible* if the forward solve is energy-based. PBD, penalty contact, and impulse methods are not differentiable in any usable sense because their step functions are not smooth; finite differences on them cost $O(p)$ forward solves per parameter but give no analytic adjoint, which is what the differentiable-physics wave committed to.

## What convergence means here

"Convergence" is shorthand for four propositions, each collapsing a previously-separate concern into a consequence of another:

- **Physical correctness and visual quality are the same fix.** [Part 1 Ch 02](../../10-physical/02-what-goes-wrong.md)'s six failure modes — volume loss, wobble, popping, missing friction, rim deformation, material poverty — are each a case where the solver's mechanical wrongness and the render's visual wrongness are the same wrongness, measured from two sides. Fixing one fixes the other. The games-branch hack layer (secondary motion, normal-map bakery, softbody-thickness offsets) was always reaching for the same fix the physics demanded, one level removed and without the benefit of the gradient.
- **Real-time and physical correctness are compatible.** GPU FEM throughput plus real-time-capable IPC means the implicit Newton loop fits a frame budget. [Phase E](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order)'s envelope is the architectural commitment; the target numbers are Warp-derived, not measurements of `sim-soft` or overclaims of what sim-soft will hit.
- **Differentiability and physical correctness are the same prerequisite.** Both require an energy-based formulation — smooth total potential plus implicit integration — because both the physics and the gradient need the same smoothness. A solver that does the physics right necessarily has a cheap-adjoint gradient path; a solver that skips the physics (PBD, penalty) cannot bolt on differentiability retroactively.
- **All four axes share one architectural skeleton.** Hyperelastic + near-incompressible + IPC + implicit Newton + GPU kernels + tape-based autograd. [Part 11 Ch 00](../../110-crate/00-module-layout.md)'s ten modules are the concrete instance; the skeleton is what makes them composable rather than a pile of separately-optimized subsystems glued together.

## The honest qualifier

Convergence is not integration. Each of the three enabling events has been demonstrated in isolation — one of [Warp](../02-sota/01-warp.md), [Genesis](../02-sota/02-genesis.md), [IPC Toolkit](../02-sota/03-ipc-toolkit.md), or [DiffTaichi](../02-sota/04-difftaichi.md) hits each individually. No shipped stack covers all four axes at once; the architectural skeleton exists, but the integrated reference implementation does not. [§02 the-gap](02-the-gap.md) names what's missing, and [Ch 02 cross-compare](../02-sota/09-cross-compare.md) scores each existing stack against the bar [§00](00-definitions.md) defines.
