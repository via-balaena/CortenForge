# Making IPC real-time

The original IPC paper (Li et al. 2020) was presented as an offline method; the canonical reference implementation, [IPC Toolkit](../00-context/02-sota/03-ipc-toolkit.md), ships Eigen-on-CPU with per-step times measured in seconds to minutes. `sim-soft` commits to real-time IPC: ≥ 30 FPS at experience-mode resolution and ≥ 5 Hz at design-mode resolution on a consumer GPU, per [Part 11 Ch 03 Phase E](../110-crate/03-build-order.md). This chapter names the three engineering levers that close the gap between 2020-offline and 2026-real-time.

| Section | Technique | Saves |
|---|---|---|
| [GPU parallelization](05-real-time/00-gpu.md) | BVH construction, primitive-pair generation, per-pair barrier evaluation, Jacobian assembly all expressed as wgpu compute kernels; per-pair work is embarrassingly parallel | 10–100× over CPU on the same algorithm; the headline speedup |
| [Adaptive barrier width](05-real-time/01-barrier-width.md) | $\hat d$ varies per pair based on local mesh resolution and approach velocity — coarser pairs get a larger tolerance, finer pairs a smaller one; reduces total pair count by culling spurious far-field contacts | 2–5× by shrinking the primitive-pair candidate set |
| [Caching strategies](05-real-time/02-caching.md) | Warm-started BVH (refit rather than rebuild across frames), persistent contact-pair lists (reuse pairs that are still active), lagged friction references | 3–10× across frames when the scene is not radically changing |

Two claims Ch 05 rests on:

1. **Real-time IPC is engineering, not new math.** The barrier form, the energy formulation, the adaptive stiffness schedule, the CCD algorithm — none of these change between the 2020 offline paper and the 2026 real-time commitment. What changes is the broad phase (GPU BVH instead of CPU), the barrier tolerance schedule (per-pair adaptive instead of globally fixed), and the across-frame warm-start (cached BVH plus cached pair lists instead of per-frame rebuild). Each of the three sub-chapters is a specific technique that has been demonstrated separately in the 2022–2024 follow-up literature; the integration of all three in a production-grade pipeline is what this Part commits `sim-soft` to.
2. **The 2026 real-time regime is a moving target, not a fixed bar.** The FPS targets in [Part 11 Ch 03 Phase E](../110-crate/03-build-order.md) are derived from [Warp's](../00-context/02-sota/01-warp.md) neo-Hookean-only benchmarks scaled to account for IPC cost; they will be revised once actual `sim-soft` benchmarks land. What is not a moving target is the commitment to *some* real-time bar — a solver that produces correct physics at 1 Hz is an offline solver, and an offline solver does not satisfy the [Ch 03 thesis](../10-physical/03-thesis.md)'s experience-mode commitment. If the three techniques here are not enough to hit the bar, [Part 12 Ch 07](../120-roadmap/07-open-questions.md) lists this as an open research question — not as a reason to drop the real-time commitment.
