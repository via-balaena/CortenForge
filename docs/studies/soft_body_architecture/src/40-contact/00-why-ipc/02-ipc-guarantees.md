# IPC guarantees

IPC — the incremental potential contact method of [Li et al. 2020](../../appendices/00-references/00-ipc.md#li-2020) — commits to four guarantees that penalty and impulse do not offer: zero penetration as a structural property rather than a numerical tolerance, $C^2$ smoothness of the total potential energy at the contact boundary, warm-start capability across Newton iterations within a timestep and across timesteps within a simulation, and differentiability through the contact barrier as a consequence of the $C^2$ smoothness. The [two sibling sub-leaves](../00-why-ipc.md) named the failure modes those guarantees refute; this sub-leaf states each guarantee precisely, gives the mechanism that produces it, and names which downstream chapter depends on it.

## Guarantee 1 — Zero penetration, structurally

Under IPC, the contact potential $b(d, \hat d) = -(d - \hat d)^2 \ln(d / \hat d)$ diverges to $+\infty$ as $d \to 0^+$. Any configuration in which a contact pair has $d \le 0$ has infinite total potential energy, so any trial Newton step whose endpoint would cross a $d = 0$ boundary fails the merit-function decrease condition and triggers backtracking to a shorter step. Li et al. 2020's *filtered line search* additionally preserves the intersection-free invariant by solving a continuous-collision-detection query for the largest step length that keeps the entire step interior non-intersecting, not just the endpoint — a stronger guarantee than Armijo decrease alone. The invariant at the start of the simulation (no penetration) is maintained as an invariant across every Newton iterate and every timestep.

This is different from a numerical tolerance in two ways. First, the non-penetration holds at every intermediate iterate, not just at the converged state — a penalty simulator may achieve near-zero penetration at convergence but pass through visibly overlapping states during Newton iteration; IPC does not. Second, the penetration-free property does not degrade with time — a penalty simulator accumulates error at each timestep under chained contact, while an IPC simulator starts each timestep from a strictly non-penetrating configuration. The [Part 4 Ch 04 multi-layer chapter](../04-multi-layer.md) is built on this invariant: stacked sleeves remain separated because the barrier makes them separated, not because a controller iterates them apart.

## Guarantee 2 — $C^2$ smooth total potential energy

The barrier is constructed so that $b(\hat d, \hat d) = 0$, $b'(\hat d, \hat d) = 0$, and $b''(\hat d, \hat d) = 0$ — the zero, first, and second derivatives all vanish at the outer edge of the tolerance band. Turning the barrier on or off as a contact pair enters or exits the tolerance $\hat d$ therefore introduces no discontinuity in energy, force, or Hessian. On the inner side of the band the barrier is $C^\infty$ (composition of polynomials and logarithm on $(0, \hat d)$); across the boundary $d = \hat d$ it is $C^2$; outside the band it is identically zero. The total potential $U(x; \theta) = \Psi_\text{elastic}(x) + \Psi_\text{contact}(x) + \Psi_\text{inertia}(x)$ is therefore $C^2$ in the nodal positions $x$ everywhere the Newton iteration visits.

This is the guarantee the Newton loop in [Part 5 Ch 00](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md) rests on — $C^2$ regularity of the potential makes the Hessian continuous, which is the prerequisite for Newton's convergence on the gradient-zero equation, and the $b''(\hat d) = 0$ boundary condition is exactly what prevents the Hessian entries from jumping discontinuously as pairs enter and leave the active set. (Quadratic convergence rate requires Lipschitz Hessian — $C^{2,1}$ regularity — which the barrier satisfies on the open interval $(0, \hat d)$ away from the boundary; the $b''(\hat d) = 0$ matching keeps the Hessian bounded across the boundary so the Lipschitz property extends through the active/inactive transition in practice.) [Penalty's discontinuous Hessian](00-penalty.md) and [impulse's piecewise solution structure](01-impulse.md) both fail at this guarantee in different ways; IPC satisfies it by construction.

## Guarantee 3 — Warm-start across Newton iterations and timesteps

Because the total potential $U(x)$ is $C^2$ and the barrier changes smoothly as positions move, a Newton iterate that closes the gap on a contact pair does not cause a discontinuous reformulation of the linear system being solved. The Hessian is updated continuously as positions evolve; warm-starting the linear inner solve (preconditioned CG or direct factorization) from the previous iterate's solution remains a well-defined operation with a predictable residual reduction per iteration.

Across timesteps, the [adaptive barrier stiffness $\kappa$ (Part 4 Ch 01)](../01-ipc-internals/01-adaptive-kappa.md) can be warm-started from the previous timestep's value. The BVH used for [self-contact pair generation (Part 4 Ch 03)](../03-self-contact.md) can be refit rather than rebuilt — vertices have moved but the tree topology and node bounds update incrementally. Persistent contact-pair lists can be warm-started because the set of active pairs changes smoothly (one pair entering or leaving per timestep in the typical case, not a wholesale active-set swap). These per-timestep warm-starts are what makes IPC tractable at real-time rates even though each individual step's Newton solve is more expensive than a penalty step; [Part 4 Ch 05 real-time](../05-real-time.md) names warm-start as one of the three engineering levers that close the 2020-offline-to-2026-real-time gap.

## Guarantee 4 — Differentiable through contact

Because $U(x; \theta)$ is $C^2$ in $x$ and at least $C^1$ in the design parameters $\theta$, the implicit-function-theorem adjoint from [Part 6 Ch 02](../../60-differentiability/02-implicit-function.md) applies at the converged equilibrium $x^\ast(\theta)$ defined by $\nabla_x U(x^\ast; \theta) = 0$:

$$ \frac{\partial x^\ast}{\partial \theta} = -\left(\frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x^2}\right)^{-1} \frac{\partial^2 U}{\partial x\, \partial \theta} $$

— the same Hessian $\partial^2 U / \partial x^2$ used in the forward Newton solve, evaluated at convergence, provides the linear operator the backward pass inverts against the sensitivity source term. The barrier's contribution to both terms is analytic and evaluable. The specific chain-rule issue that arises from the $\ln(d/\hat d)$ term — where a naive autograd expansion produces divergent pieces that cancel to a finite gradient — is handled by a [custom VJP in Part 6 Ch 01](../../60-differentiability/01-custom-vjps.md) that rearranges the derivation to avoid the cancellation; this is noted here and deferred to that sub-chapter for the mechanics.

The guarantee is not that IPC is magically more differentiable than other formulations — it is that IPC satisfies the prerequisite (smooth total potential) that the adjoint method needs. Penalty and impulse do not satisfy the prerequisite, so no adjoint infrastructure makes them differentiable in a usable sense; IPC does, so the existing adjoint infrastructure just works.

## The four guarantees are one property, stated four times

The four guarantees are not independent. $C^2$ smoothness of the total potential (Guarantee 2) directly implies Newton convergence (used in Guarantee 3), non-penetration via filtered line search on a divergent barrier (Guarantee 1 — the divergence to $+\infty$ is what makes the smooth potential unbounded as $d \to 0$, which is what makes the energy minimizer unable to cross the contact boundary and thus makes non-penetration structural), and IFT-adjoint differentiability (Guarantee 4 — $C^2$ is the regularity the IFT requires). The barrier's specific form with $b = b' = b'' = 0$ at $d = \hat d$ is what unifies all four.

The [Part 4 Ch 01 ipc-internals chapter](../01-ipc-internals.md) walks the four components that produce this single property — the barrier function, the adaptive-$\kappa$ schedule, the CCD, and the energy-based formulation. This sub-leaf is the "what IPC gives you" half of the story; Ch 01 is the "how it works" half.

## What this sub-leaf commits the book to

- **Zero penetration is not a tolerance parameter.** `sim-soft` does not expose a "penetration tolerance" knob; the barrier-width $\hat d$ is an engagement-distance parameter, not a penetration-depth parameter. Penetration depth is structurally zero. The [Part 11 Ch 00 element module](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/01-element.md) API reflects this — no penetration-depth query exists on the contact-pair interface.
- **The $C^2$ total potential is the interface between this chapter and everything downstream.** [Part 5's Newton solver](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md), [Part 6's IFT adjoint](../../60-differentiability/02-implicit-function.md), and [Part 8's sparse Hessian assembly](../../80-gpu/01-sparse-matrix/01-bsr.md) all assume the total potential is $C^2$. The barrier form is chosen to make that assumption hold; changing the barrier form would propagate a re-proof requirement through three downstream parts.
- **Differentiability is a consequence of smoothness, not a goal in its own right.** The [Part 1 Ch 03 thesis](../../10-physical/03-thesis.md)'s "differentiability as a free byproduct of doing the physics right" is specifically this implication: smooth physics → smooth total potential → IFT-adjoint applies → gradients exist. The gradient infrastructure is not bolted onto the physics; it is what the physics already supports.
