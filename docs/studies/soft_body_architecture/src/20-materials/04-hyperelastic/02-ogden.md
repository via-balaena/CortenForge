# Ogden

Ogden is `sim-soft`'s high-strain hyperelastic law, the outermost tier in the [Part 2 Ch 04 — hyperelastic family](../04-hyperelastic.md) selector table. Unlike [neo-Hookean](00-neo-hookean.md) and [Mooney-Rivlin](01-mooney-rivlin.md), which are formulated as functions of the isotropic invariants $I_1, I_2, I_3$, Ogden is formulated directly in the principal stretches $\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3$ — the eigenvalues of the right stretch tensor $U$ from the polar decomposition $F = RU$, per [Part 2 Ch 01 — strain measures](../01-strain-measures.md). The three sub-chapters below walk the general $N$-term form (Ogden 1972), the specific reason it dominates Ecoflex curve fits at high strain, and the numerical-stability treatment `sim-soft` needs to ship it.

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [N-term form](02-ogden/00-n-term.md) | $\psi = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \tfrac{\mu_i}{\alpha_i}\left(\lambda_1^{\alpha_i} + \lambda_2^{\alpha_i} + \lambda_3^{\alpha_i} - 3\right) + U_\text{vol}(J)$; principal-stretch derivatives for first Piola and tangent; typical $N = 2$ or $3$ |
| [Why it dominates for Ecoflex at high strain](02-ogden/01-why-ogden.md) | Non-integer $\alpha_i$ exponents — including negative ones — produce the asymmetric stiffening shape Ecoflex shows at high stretch, which $I_1$-only laws cannot represent |
| [Numerical stability](02-ogden/02-stability.md) | Non-integer `pow` versus integer-power inlining; the isotropic-point ($\lambda_1 \approx \lambda_2 \approx \lambda_3$) degeneracy where principal-stretch derivatives are ill-conditioned; `sim-soft`'s perturbation strategy |

Four claims the Ogden branch rests on:

1. **The principal-stretch formulation is a structurally different law, not just a bigger fit.** With non-integer or negative $\alpha_i$, the term $\lambda_i^{\alpha_i}$ is not a rational function of $I_1, I_2, I_3$, so the Ogden energy reaches function shapes that neo-Hookean, Mooney-Rivlin, and their higher-order polynomial cousins cannot reach at any truncation. This is why a two-term ($N{=}2$) Ogden fit matches silicone data through regimes where a two-parameter Mooney-Rivlin fit saturates: the Ogden degrees of freedom track physical features (chain extensibility, compression stiffening) rather than being projected through a low-order invariant basis.

2. **$N = 2$ or $N = 3$ is the practical range; more terms overfit.** At $N = 2$, Ogden captures most silicone stress–strain curves through their strain-hardening regime. At $N = 3$, it also captures the extra inflection near the elongation-at-break limit that Ecoflex shows in published characterization data. Beyond $N = 3$, the fit becomes underconstrained against any realistic calibration dataset — extra terms trade each other off on the residual without picking out physical features. The [material database](../../appendices/02-material-db.md) reserves an $N = 3$ row for each silicone entry as a Pass-3 deliverable; `sim-soft` supports general $N$ but calibrated defaults ship only for $N = 2$ and $N = 3$.

3. **The compression-stiffening shape is where Ogden wins over Mooney-Rivlin.** Ecoflex under contact-driven loading sees both tensile stretching at the rim of a [compliant cavity](../../10-physical/00-canonical.md) and substantial compression at the indentation zone, and the measured stress response is not symmetric about $\lambda = 1$. Ogden's freedom to choose both positive and negative $\alpha_i$ lets a single model capture the asymmetric stiffening on both sides of the reference configuration; [the why-Ogden sub-chapter](02-ogden/01-why-ogden.md) walks this against published Ecoflex data. [Mooney-Rivlin](01-mooney-rivlin.md)'s $I_1, I_2$ polynomial form cannot express the asymmetry at any coefficient choice — which is the structural reason it saturates above the moderate-stretch regime.

4. **The cost is two-fold: non-integer `pow` and isotropic-point ill-conditioning.** Evaluating $\lambda^{\alpha}$ for non-integer $\alpha$ is substantially slower than integer-power inlining on modern CPUs — the hot path becomes a `pow` per stretch per Gauss point per term — and both the first Piola stress and the tangent need the principal-stretch derivatives $\partial \lambda_i / \partial F$, which become ill-conditioned as $\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \lambda_3$ converge because the eigenvectors of $U$ are only defined up to a rotation in the degenerate subspace. The [numerical-stability sub-chapter](02-ogden/02-stability.md) commits to an $\varepsilon$-level perturbation that splits near-repeated eigenvalues, with a fallback to the invariant-based equivalent form when the perturbation would dominate — the `impl Material` never hands a hard degeneracy back to the Newton loop of [Part 5 Ch 00](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md).
