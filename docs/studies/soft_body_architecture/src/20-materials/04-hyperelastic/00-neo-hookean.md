# Neo-Hookean

Neo-Hookean is `sim-soft`'s default hyperelastic law, per the selector table in [Part 2 Ch 04 — hyperelastic family](../04-hyperelastic.md). It takes two parameters — the Lamé pair $(\mu, \lambda)$, equivalently $(E, \nu)$ — and evaluates energy, first Piola stress, and fourth-order tangent at each Gauss point in closed form. The four sub-chapters below split the derivation into its pieces: the energy-density and stress expressions, the tangent, the near-incompressible rearrangement that hands off to [Ch 05's locking treatments](../05-incompressibility.md), and the Rust `impl Material` that the `material/` module actually ships.

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [Energy density and stress](00-neo-hookean/00-energy.md) | $\psi(F) = \tfrac{\mu}{2}(I_1 - 3) - \mu \ln J + \tfrac{\lambda}{2}(\ln J)^2$ with $I_1 = \mathrm{tr}(F^T F)$ and $J = \det F$; first Piola $P = \partial \psi / \partial F = \mu(F - F^{-T}) + \lambda(\ln J)\, F^{-T}$ |
| [Tangent stiffness](00-neo-hookean/01-tangent.md) | The fourth-order $\partial P / \partial F$ at each Gauss point as a closed-form tensor in $F^{-T}$, $I$, and the Lamé pair; sparse assembly of the 12×12 (Tet4) and 30×30 (Tet10) per-element blocks |
| [Near-incompressible form](00-neo-hookean/02-incompressible.md) | Volumetric/deviatoric split, mixed u-p alternative as $\nu \to 0.5$; the handoff to [Ch 05 incompressibility](../05-incompressibility.md) for the locking fix |
| [Implementation pseudocode](00-neo-hookean/03-impl.md) | `impl Material for NeoHookean` with `nalgebra::Matrix3` hot path, validity-domain declaration, hooks for the [gradcheck suite](../../110-crate/04-testing/03-gradcheck.md) |

Four claims the neo-Hookean branch rests on:

1. **Two parameters are enough in the canonical problem's regime.** Neo-Hookean's $(\mu, \lambda)$ calibrate cleanly from either a manufacturer data sheet (Shore hardness to $E$ via the standard correlation, then $\mu = E / 2(1+\nu)$) or a single uniaxial curve up to the onset of strain hardening. For the silicones the book cares about — Ecoflex and Dragon Skin below their breakdown stretch, per [Part 1 Ch 04 — material data](../../10-physical/04-material-data.md) — this fit tracks the measured curve to within a few percent up to roughly 100% stretch, and the cost of stepping up to [Mooney-Rivlin](01-mooney-rivlin.md) or [Ogden](02-ogden.md) is an expanded calibration set the designer may not have. The [appendices material database](../../appendices/02-material-db.md) ships neo-Hookean $\mu$ for every Ecoflex and Dragon Skin row and defers the Ogden coefficient sets to Pass 3 by design.

2. **Rotation-invariance is structural, not bolted on.** The energy is a function of $I_1$ and $J$, both invariant under $F \mapsto R F$ for any $R \in SO(3)$, so a rigidly rotated configuration produces zero extra stress by construction. This closes the fictitious-stress failure mode from [Ch 02 linear elasticity](../02-linear.md) that [Ch 03 corotational](../03-corotational.md) only papers over via an extracted polar decomposition. It is also the structural reason neo-Hookean is the Phase B solver-plumbing target in [Part 11 Ch 03](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md) — the rotation-invariance gradcheck every `Material` must pass falls out of the invariant formulation rather than having to be engineered around it.

3. **The tangent is closed-form and cheap.** $\partial P / \partial F$ at each Gauss point is a fourth-order tensor built from $F^{-T}$, $I$, and the two Lamé scalars — no numerical differentiation, no linearization around a previous step. Sparse assembly of the per-element 12×12 (Tet4) or 30×30 (Tet10) stiffness block is a direct fan-out into the global Newton tangent (per [Part 3 Ch 00 — element choice](../../30-discretization/00-element-choice.md)). That cheapness is what makes the Backward-Euler + Newton outer loop of [Part 5 Ch 00](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md) practical at canonical-problem mesh sizes and what pins the Phase B gradcheck target the rest of the physics inherits.

4. **The $(\ln J)^2$ term hardens against inversion but does not fix locking.** At silicone's $\nu \approx 0.499$ the ratio $\lambda / \mu$ blows up, which is the driving cause of the volumetric-locking failure named in [Ch 05 — incompressibility](../05-incompressibility.md). The $\tfrac{\lambda}{2}(\ln J)^2$ volumetric term is well-conditioned against $J \to 0^+$ element inversion — this matters for IPC-guarded deformations that swing close to degenerate configurations — but it is not a locking cure. The near-incompressible sub-chapter hands off to Ch 05's mixed u-p treatment for that; the point at the branch level is that neo-Hookean is a constitutive law, not a locking fix, and the two concerns live in adjacent chapters rather than inside this one.
