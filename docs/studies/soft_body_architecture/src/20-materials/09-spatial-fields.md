# Spatial material fields from SDFs

This chapter is where the [Ch 03 thesis commitment](../10-physical/03-thesis.md) to SDF-valued material fields materializes. A constant-stiffness uniform soft body is the degenerate case; a realistic soft part has material parameters that vary across the volume — stiffness graded from a soft shell to a stiff core, fiber direction following a braided pattern, density stepping at layer interfaces. The thesis said material is a field, not a constant. This chapter says how.

| Section | Topic | Role |
|---|---|---|
| [SDF-valued material parameters](09-spatial-fields/00-sdf-valued.md) | Each scalar material parameter is a signed-distance field or a more general implicit function over reference space; typed material fields (unit-vector for fiber, tensor for full orthotropy) generalize the scalar case | The public API for material-field specification; `cf-design` produces these |
| [Per-element sampling](09-spatial-fields/01-sampling.md) | Tet centroid evaluation as the default; Gauss-point sampling for higher accuracy; piecewise-constant-per-element vs. linearly-interpolated from vertex samples | The bridge from continuous fields to discrete element properties |
| [Gradient flow through SDF evaluation](09-spatial-fields/02-gradient-flow.md) | The reward gradient $\partial R / \partial \theta$ flows through the SDF evaluation at every sampled element; SDF parameters $\theta$ (spheroid radii, blending weights, layer boundaries) enter the differentiable pipeline at this point | How `cf-design` design parameters become optimization variables in the [ml-chassis loop](../100-optimization/00-forward.md) |

Two claims Ch 09 rests on:

1. **Material is a field, not a constant.** A Young's modulus of 500 kPa everywhere is a special case; a realistic soft part has modulus varying smoothly (molded gradients, dip-coat layer transitions) or abruptly (bonded multi-material). The `Material` trait's scalar parameters (the $\mu, \lambda$ of neo-Hookean, the $\{(\mu_i, \alpha_i)\}$ of Ogden, the $k_1, k_2$ and fiber direction of HGO, the Prony parameters, the thermal expansion coefficient) all become `Sdf<f64>` or `Sdf<UnitVec3>` fields, sampled per element. This is a generalization at the type level, not a runtime branch — a uniform field is a constant `Sdf`; a graded field is a more complex `Sdf`; the solver does not know or care.
2. **Differentiability is per-sample.** The optimizer in [Part 10](../100-optimization/00-forward.md) wants to adjust SDF parameters (layer boundary positions, fillet radii, material-blend weights) and see the reward move. The gradient chain is: reward depends on per-element stress depends on per-element material evaluation depends on SDF-sampled parameters depends on SDF scalar parameters. At every link, the gradient flows by straightforward reverse-mode autograd — no custom VJP needed *unless* the SDF evaluation goes through meshing, which is [Part 6 Ch 05's open problem](../60-differentiability/05-diff-meshing.md). The material-field gradient is the easy part; the meshing-gradient is the hard part. Ch 09 lives on the easy side of that line.
