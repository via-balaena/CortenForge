# Viscoelasticity

Silicones are viscoelastic. Step-loaded, they creep; oscillated, they dissipate; released from a strained state, they relax exponentially toward equilibrium. Without a viscoelastic term, the solver reproduces the [wobble failure from Part 1 Ch 02](../10-physical/02-what-goes-wrong/01-wobble.md) — under-damped oscillations at the mesh's natural frequencies that a real silicone never exhibits. This chapter names the constitutive machinery that fixes it and the measurement protocol that calibrates it.

| Section | Topic | Role |
|---|---|---|
| [Prony series — discrete relaxation spectrum](07-viscoelastic/00-prony.md) | $N$ Maxwell branches in parallel with an elastic spring, each branch with stiffness $E_i$ and relaxation time $\tau_i$; discrete approximation to the continuous relaxation spectrum | The default viscoelastic representation in `sim-soft` — directly fittable from DMA data |
| [Oldroyd-B for large deformation](07-viscoelastic/01-oldroyd.md) | Frame-indifferent upper-convected-derivative form; correct under large rotations and stretches where small-strain linear viscoelasticity fails | For regimes where Prony's small-strain-linearity assumption is violated |
| [Time-dependent tangent stiffness](07-viscoelastic/02-tangent.md) | The tangent operator $\partial P / \partial F$ for viscoelastic materials depends on the timestep $\Delta t$ through the history integration; the Newton-solver tangent changes shape accordingly | Implementation-level consequence for the [solver](../110-crate/00-module-layout/04-solver.md) |
| [DMA measurement protocol](07-viscoelastic/03-dma.md) | Dynamic mechanical analysis — sinusoidal small-strain excitation, storage and loss modulus versus frequency; the Prony parameters fit to these curves | Where the numeric parameters come from |

Two claims Ch 07 rests on:

1. **Viscoelasticity is a time-axis extension of elasticity, not a separate law.** An elastic `Material` gives stress from current $F$; a viscoelastic `Material` gives stress from current $F$ *plus* internal history variables that evolve with a first-order ODE in time. The [Material trait](00-trait-hierarchy.md) handles this via a `Viscoelastic<M: Material>` decorator composition ([Ch 00 sub-chapter 01](00-trait-hierarchy/01-composition.md)) that wraps a hyperelastic core with per-element history state. The hyperelastic core is untouched; the wrapper adds the Prony machinery. The history variables are stored in the per-element data block alongside $F$, live through the autograd tape, and restart cleanly on [warm re-mesh](../70-sdf-pipeline/04-live-remesh/01-warm-start.md).
2. **Prony series is the Ockham's-razor parametrization.** Continuous viscoelastic spectra require infinitely many parameters; Prony discretizes with $N$ branches, typically $N = 3$ or $4$ for silicones. DMA measurements produce the storage and loss modulus curves; nonlinear least-squares fits the Prony parameters to those curves; the fit error and the number of branches are each validity-domain descriptors on the `Viscoelastic` `Material`. More exotic viscoelastic laws (Oldroyd-B, fractional-derivative models) exist for specific regimes — large-deformation flows, polymer melts — and are treated as specializations, not the default.
