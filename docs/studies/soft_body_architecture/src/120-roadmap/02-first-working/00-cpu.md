# Neo-Hookean + IPC on CPU

At Phase D close, `sim-soft` closes the first of [Ch 02's two first-working guarantees](../02-first-working.md): on the [canonical compliant-cavity-and-probe](../../10-physical/00-canonical.md), the Newton loop converges to static equilibrium in a few iterations, per-step wall time lands inside the Phase D CPU budget, and the IPC barrier holds no-penetration through contact. Every module from [Part 11 Ch 00's module layout](../../110-crate/00-module-layout.md) that the forward solve touches — `material/`, `mesh/`, `element/`, `contact/`, `solver/`, `readout/`, `autograd/` — is live on CPU. `gpu/`, `sdf_bridge/`, and `coupling/` are not. The deliverable is numeric convergence on the canonical problem, not a rendered frame.

## What Phase D's CPU forward solve guarantees

- **Neo-Hookean constitutive on [`material/`](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/00-material.md).** The [Part 2 Ch 04 neo-Hookean strain-energy density](../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic.md) is the baseline `Material` impl. Linear-elastic and corotational from [Part 2 Ch 02](../../20-materials/02-linear.md) and [Part 2 Ch 03](../../20-materials/03-corotational.md) are live alongside as mid-point constitutive laws for regression comparison; the canonical sleeve-on-probe runs against neo-Hookean.
- **Tet4 elements on [`element/`](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/01-element.md).** The `Element` impl specialized for a four-node tetrahedron with a single-Gauss-point quadrature rule, via the const-generic `<const N, const G>` parameters from [Part 11 Ch 01 core traits](../../110-crate/01-traits/00-core.md). Tet10 is [Phase H](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order).
- **IPC barrier + CCD + friction on [`contact/`](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/03-contact.md).** [Part 4 Ch 00's no-penetration commitment](../../40-contact/00-why-ipc.md) holds through every Newton iterate, not just at convergence. Continuous collision detection computes the maximum admissible step; smoothed Coulomb friction from [Part 4 Ch 02](../../40-contact/02-friction.md) keeps the tangential-force gradient defined. Contact integration into the Newton loop was landed at [Phase C](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order); Phase D keeps it running under the full `readout/`-closed forward path.
- **Backward-Euler Newton on [`solver/`](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/04-solver.md).** One implicit step per canonical-problem frame, typically 2–3 Newton iterations per step, each iteration reusing the [Part 5 Ch 00 faer `Llt<f64>` factor-on-tape pattern](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md). The factorization is a first-class tape artifact — Phase D installs the pattern, Phases E–I pay it off.
- **Reward readout on [`readout/`](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/09-readout.md).** The γ-locked [`RewardBreakdown`](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) per-term struct surfaces pressure uniformity, coverage, peak-pressure barrier, and effective-stiffness from converged equilibrium $x^\ast$ and $\theta$, per [Part 1 Ch 01's reward composition](../../10-physical/01-reward.md). Scalar composition via `breakdown.score_with(&weights)` is performed by the optimizer on the other side of the [`sim-ml-chassis` coupling](../../110-crate/02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md); `sim-soft` does not expose a scalar.
- **`ForwardMap` trait live end-to-end.** The γ-locked [`ForwardMap::evaluate(theta, tape) -> (RewardBreakdown, EditResult)`](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) lands here. Phase D is the first phase at which the full trait contract binds the forward path. `EditResult` exercises only the `ParameterOnly` cost regime because no SDF bridge is live — geometry comes from a hardcoded scene rather than a `cf-design` edit — so the three-class classifier sits on the return type but stays dormant in practice until [Phase G](../04-sdf.md).

## The canonical problem at Phase D

- **Scene.** The canonical compliant-cavity-and-probe at Phase D's tractable size — the 100-tet regression fixture named in [Part 11 Ch 04 §03](../../110-crate/04-testing/03-gradcheck.md) plus small synthetic-design trials. The 30k-tet design-mode resolution from [Part 10 Ch 00's cost table](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) is [Phase E's GPU target](../03-gpu.md), not Phase D's achievement.
- **Per-step wall time.** The Phase D CPU budget is whatever keeps the synthetic 2-parameter BayesOpt loop from [Ch 06's phase-1 optimizer](../06-optimization.md) iterable in unit-test time — seconds per step on the small regression scene, not minutes. The [cost-table structure](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) is instantiated at Phase D via the `EditResult` return type; [Phase E](../03-gpu.md) fills in the 30k-tet wall-time numbers, and [Phase G](../04-sdf.md) populates the `MaterialChanging` and `TopologyChanging` rows once the [`cf-design` coupling](../../110-crate/02-coupling/04-cf-design.md) drives live edits.
- **Design space.** A synthetic 2-parameter cut — small enough that finite differences are tractable as a debugging tool, rich enough that the reward is not trivially linear. Phase D's optimizer runs [Ch 06's phase-1 BayesOpt](../06-optimization.md) against this space; the 10–50-parameter canonical design space from [Part 10 Ch 00](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) is a Phase G deliverable when `cf-design` authoring goes live.

## What Phase D does not yet deliver

- **No GPU.** Phase E. The forward solve runs on CPU; parallelism and the 30-FPS experience-mode target come in with [the `gpu/` wgpu port](../03-gpu.md).
- **No live SDF authoring.** Phase G. Mesh geometry is fixed at scene construction; the [three-class `EditResult` classifier](../04-sdf.md) is on the trait surface but never observes anything but `ParameterOnly` edits.
- **No `sim-core` or `sim-thermostat` coupling.** Phase F. The rigid probe is a hardcoded geometry, not a [`sim-core` body](../../110-crate/02-coupling/00-mjcf.md) with its own rigid-body dynamics; temperature fields are not fed from [`sim-thermostat`](../../110-crate/02-coupling/01-thermostat.md).
- **No visual layer beyond diagnostic.** Phase I. A Phase D demo is a terminal printing converged residuals, reward-term values, and gradient components — not a [rendered silicone sleeve](../05-visual.md). [Part 11 Ch 03's Phase D defense](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#why-this-order) sets the terms: an ugly Tet4-no-subdivision render is worse than no render.
- **No sim-to-real correction.** Post-Phase-I per [Ch 07's open-questions housing](../07-open-questions.md). The [`SimToRealCorrection` boundary type](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) exists in the `sim-opt` API but is dormant — residual GPs are initialized with zero training data, and no [`MeasurementReport` ingestion](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) is wired up. Phase D's correction is the identity map.

## Why Phase D is the right "first working" line

Two alternative phase boundaries suggest themselves and are rejected by [Part 11 Ch 03's build-order defense](../../110-crate/03-build-order.md#why-this-order).

**Phase C close — contact working.** At Phase C close the canonical problem runs to equilibrium under squeeze, contact pressure is computed, and interpenetration is held off. But `readout/` does not yet exist as a module, and the reward has no structured form at the `ForwardMap` surface — the [`sim-ml-chassis` coupling](../../110-crate/02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md) is not closed. An optimizer cannot consume Phase C's solver directly. "Working" at Phase C means a physics simulation runs to equilibrium; it does not mean a design loop closes end-to-end.

**Phase E close — GPU-interactive.** At Phase E the canonical problem runs at interactive rates. But the Phase D CPU path is the regression oracle for Phase E's GPU port per [`gpu/` module layout](../../110-crate/00-module-layout/06-gpu.md); a "first working" line at Phase E would elide the CPU-path deliverable the rest of the project leans on. [Ch 03's gpu milestone](../03-gpu.md) is "first interactive `sim-soft`," a distinct claim from "first working `sim-soft`."

Phase D is the line where the `sim-soft` → `sim-ml-chassis` → `sim-opt` optimizer loop closes at all — first on a synthetic 2-parameter space, later on the real canonical space when Phase G lands `cf-design` coupling. Closing the loop at any scale is what "first working" names.
