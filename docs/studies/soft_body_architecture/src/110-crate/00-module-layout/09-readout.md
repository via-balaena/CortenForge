# readout/

The `readout/` module is `sim-soft`'s observation layer: it extracts per-tet stress, contact pressure, per-vertex temperature, and the reward terms of [Part 1 Ch 01](../../10-physical/01-reward.md) from a converged Newton step; composes the terms into a [`RewardBreakdown`](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) struct; and surfaces the [`ForwardMap` trait](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) that every consumer crate ([`sim-opt`](../02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md), [`sim-ml-chassis`](../02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md), [`sim-bevy`](../02-coupling/02-bevy.md)) sees. This is the last module in the crate's internal dependency order and the closest one to the downstream optimizer â€” everything `sim-soft` emits to the outside world flows through here.

## Four claims

**1. `ForwardMap` and `RewardBreakdown` are Îł-locked API.** [Part 10 Ch 00](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) carries the signatures; this module is the implementation. `ForwardMap::evaluate(theta, tape) -> (RewardBreakdown, EditResult)` produces the four-term breakdown as a struct, not a scalar â€” because [`sim-opt`'s `SimToRealCorrection::correct`](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) consumes per-component residuals, not a pre-composed scalar. The composition to a scalar reward happens via `breakdown.score_with(&weights)` under the [Part 1 Ch 01 Â§04 composition rule](../../10-physical/01-reward/04-composition.md). Consumers that only want a scalar call `score_with`; consumers that want the breakdown (residual-GP updates, preference elicitation on per-term weights) read the struct directly. Changing the field list on `RewardBreakdown` â€” adding a fifth term, renaming one â€” is a cross-crate edit, not a local `sim-soft` edit.

**2. Determinism-in-Î¸ is a module-level invariant.** [Part 10 Ch 00's `ForwardMap`](../../100-optimization/00-forward.md) commits that repeated `evaluate` calls at the same `theta` produce the same `RewardBreakdown` modulo cache state â€” no stochastic sim-parameter updates between calls. `readout/` is the module where this guarantee cashes out for the reward: stress extraction is closed-form over the converged $x^\ast$, contact pressure is closed-form over active pairs, reward terms are closed-form over the stress and pressure fields. No RNG, no timestamped state, no per-call-counter drift. The upstream solver's determinism-in-Î¸ ([`solver/`](04-solver.md) + [`autograd/`](07-autograd.md)) plus this module's closed-form readout chain together give the end-to-end invariant [Part 10 Ch 02's BayesOpt GP cache](../../100-optimization/02-bayesopt/00-gp.md) and [Part 10 Ch 05's residual-GP online updates](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real/01-online.md) rely on.

**3. `RewardBreakdown::apply_residuals` formalizes the sim-to-real correction seam.** [Part 10 Ch 05 Â§00](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real/00-calibration.md) introduces a method signature â€” `r_corrected = r_sim.apply_residuals(&force_delta, &pressure_delta, &diffusion_delta, &stiffness_delta)` â€” that composes per-component GP-predicted residuals into a corrected breakdown. The method is named in [Part 10 Ch 05](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) but was never upstream-introduced in the Part 10 chain. `readout/` is the formalization home: the method is part of the `RewardBreakdown` impl that this module exports, `sim-soft` owns the type and its component-wise residual composition, and [`sim-opt`'s `SimToRealCorrection::correct`](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) is the caller. The alternative placement â€” promoting to [Part 1 Ch 01 Â§04 composition](../../10-physical/01-reward/04-composition.md) as a composition rule â€” was considered and rejected: Part 1 is the reward-definition chapter, not the sim-to-real-correction chapter, and the method is specifically about applying residuals that only [`sim-opt`](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) computes. This module owns the method; the decision is explicit here rather than deferred.

**4. [`sim-bevy`](../02-coupling/02-bevy.md)'s visual layer reads the same attributes.** The per-vertex stress / temperature / contact-pressure fields this module extracts for the reward are also the shader inputs [Part 9](../../90-visual/00-sss.md) needs â€” micro-wrinkles from contact pressure ([Part 9 Ch 02 Â§00](../../90-visual/02-micro-wrinkles/00-contact-normals.md)), thermal overlays from the temperature field ([Part 9 Ch 04 Â§01](../../90-visual/04-thermal-viz/01-overlays.md)), stress-driven material variation ([Part 9 Ch 00](../../90-visual/00-sss.md)). One extraction pass feeds both reward and rendering; this is the concrete shape of [Part 1 Ch 03's](../../10-physical/03-thesis.md) "the visual configuration is a shader, not a second simulation."

## What the module does not carry

- **No optimizer state.** [`sim-opt`](../02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md) owns `GradientEnhancedGP`, `PreferenceGP`, `BradleyTerryGP`, `Acquisition` variants, `SimToRealCorrection`, `MeasurementReport`, `OnlineUpdateOutcome`. `readout/` produces the inputs those optimizers consume; it does not consume them itself.
- **No shader pipeline.** [`sim-bevy`](../02-coupling/02-bevy.md)'s shader authoring lives in that crate ([Part 9 Ch 05 Â§01](../../90-visual/05-sim-bevy/01-shader-pipeline.md)). `readout/` streams attributes; shaders read them.
- **No reward-weight learning.** Preference learning updates the reward-composition weights $w_i$ but does so via [`sim-opt`'s `PreferenceGP`](../../100-optimization/03-preference/00-gp-pairs.md), not via mutation inside `readout/`. This module reads the current weights from the `ForwardMap`'s state; it does not decide what they are.
- **No per-step tape recording.** Reward evaluation is recorded by the chassis's tape via [`autograd/`](07-autograd.md)'s registered VJPs for the closed-form readout terms. `readout/` supplies the forward expressions; `autograd/` owns their gradient machinery.

## What this commits downstream

- **[`sim-opt`](../02-coupling/03-ml-chassis.md)'s `SimToRealCorrection::correct(theta, r_sim) -> RewardBreakdown`** consumes this module's `RewardBreakdown`. The Îł-locked `SimToRealCorrection` struct ([Part 10 Ch 05](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md)) is the direct caller of `apply_residuals`.
- **[Part 10 Ch 05 `MeasurementReport`](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md)** is the sibling-side boundary type â€” `sim-opt` consumes `MeasurementReport` from the physical-print loop, ingests it to update residual GPs, and corrects `readout/`'s sim rewards with the result. `MeasurementReport` never crosses into `sim-soft`; the only cross-module type is `RewardBreakdown`.
- **[Part 11 Ch 04 regression tests](../04-testing/01-regression.md)** assert stress fields, contact-pressure curves, and per-term reward values against MuJoCo-flex baselines on the canonical problem; the regressions bind on this module's outputs, not on the raw solver state.
- **[Part 11 Ch 03 Phase D deliverable](../03-build-order.md#the-committed-order)** â€” the "first working `sim-soft`" â€” closes when this module's `ForwardMap` can evaluate a two-parameter design on CPU end-to-end through IFT gradients. Every other module ships before this one, and this one is what turns the assembled crate into a thing optimizers can point at.
