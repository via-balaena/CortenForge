# Differentiable marching cubes — Deep MC and Neural MC

Two published research lines attempt to make marching cubes itself differentiable rather than working around the topology-classification step [§00](00-why-not-mc.md) names. The first — **Deep Marching Cubes** ([Liao, Donné & Geiger 2018](../../appendices/00-references/03-diff-sim.md#liao-2018)) — converts the discrete topology choice into a probability distribution so the whole pipeline composes under autograd. The second — **Neural Marching Cubes** ([Chen & Zhang 2021](../../appendices/00-references/03-diff-sim.md#chen-zhang-2021)) — keeps the topology lookup discrete but amortizes the choice of the lookup table into learned weights. Both have been demonstrated end-to-end for inverse-rendering and shape-reconstruction tasks. Neither produces a mesh that a production FEM solver can consume unmodified. This sub-leaf summarizes what they do and where they break as `sim-soft` drop-ins.

## Deep marching cubes — soft topology as expectation

Liao 2018 predicts two quantities per grid cell: a **per-corner occupancy probability** $o_i \in [0,1]$ (a sigmoid of a learned score per corner, not a softmax over topology patterns) and a **continuous vertex displacement** on each edge. The eight independent occupancies induce a distribution over the $2^8 = 256$ possible sign configurations, which factors through the classical Lorensen & Cline 1987 lookup table into a distribution over topology patterns. The mesh loss (point-to-mesh distance, smoothness, curvature) is computed as an *expectation* under this distribution.

The mechanism side-steps the topology-classification Dirac: instead of differentiating through a hard choice, Liao differentiates through a weighted sum over *all* choices. As the occupancy probabilities sharpen during training toward binary values, the expected mesh approaches the classical marching-cubes output; in the intermediate regime, mass is spread across multiple topologies simultaneously.

Two structural consequences:

- **Phantom vertices and fractional connectivity during training.** Before the occupancies saturate, the mesh has non-zero probability mass on configurations that disagree about which edges carry vertices and which tets the configuration induces. The "mesh" at that point is not a single $(V, T)$ pair but a distribution over them. Downstream consumers that can handle that distribution (a point-cloud Chamfer loss, a differentiable silhouette renderer) work; downstream consumers that assume a single $(V, T)$ pair (FEM stiffness assembly, IPC contact) do not.
- **Sharpening depends on training dynamics, not a temperature schedule.** Liao does not introduce an explicit temperature parameter; the sharpening happens because the downstream loss rewards occupancy predictions consistent with the target surface. A `sim-soft` deployment that wants sharp topology would either need a compatible downstream loss or an explicit temperature-annealing schedule bolted on — the latter is a well-known pattern in Gumbel-softmax-style discrete-latent optimization, but it is not what Liao 2018 ships.

For `sim-soft` the second consequence is the blocking issue. An FEM solve requires a fully-specified mesh at every optimizer step, not a distribution over meshes, and the stiffness assembly of [Ch 01 §01](../01-custom-vjps/01-fem-assembly.md) has no meaningful definition on a fractional-connectivity input. Phantom mass from fractionally-occupied cells would show up as phantom inertia in the backward-Euler [Ch 00 §00](../../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler/00-why-implicit.md) and corrupt the dynamics. A training schedule that forces occupancies to saturate before the FEM solve runs recovers a discrete mesh — but then the topology-change gradients vanish exactly where `sim-soft` needs them most (design-mode edits that cross a topology threshold), reducing the gain to zero.

## Neural marching cubes — amortized discrete choice

Chen & Zhang 2021 commits to keeping the topology choice discrete but learns a *replacement* for Lorensen & Cline's fixed tessellation lookup table. The network produces two outputs per cell: a **binary per-corner sign prediction** (interpreted as inside/outside classification, not a continuous occupancy) and **continuous vertex-position offsets** on the edges that the chosen topology declares crossed.

The binary sign pattern indexes a learned lookup table with 37 unique topological cases — substantially more than the 15 rotational equivalence classes the classical Lorensen & Cline lookup keeps, because the neural variant distinguishes cases the classical reduction merges so the learned vertex-placement has more templates to draw from. Topology selection at inference is a deterministic table lookup on the binary output; the differentiability bought by this approach is entirely in the *vertex-position offsets*, which flow smoothly back to the SDF. The "amortized discrete choice" framing means the learned template set is trained offline against a ground-truth reconstruction objective, and the online cost at inference is one lookup plus the smooth vertex-placement adjoint.

This is a different trade-off from Liao 2018. Chen & Zhang produces a fully-specified discrete mesh at every forward, so there are no phantom vertices and no fractional connectivity. The cost is that **no gradient flows through topology changes** — the binary sign prediction is itself discrete, and sweeping an SDF parameter across a sign flip produces the same step discontinuity [§00](00-why-not-mc.md) identified. The method buys smoother vertex placement at a fixed topology; it does not buy topology-change gradients.

For reconstruction tasks — where the "right topology" is dictated by a ground-truth surface in the training set and the network's job is to reproduce it — this is fine; the discrete choice is what the supervised loss is pushing the network to get right. For `sim-soft` the analogous statement would be: the right topology is dictated by the *downstream physics*, but the book does not have a supervised training signal for it. Even if one constructed such a signal (a reward gradient back-propagated into the meshing network), the topology-change Dirac would re-emerge at the network's output layer; the amortization relocates the discontinuity but does not remove it.

## Why neither is a production FEM drop-in

The two methods delimit the space of soft-topology-MC approaches reasonably well. Liao 2018's direction — make the topology itself continuous — produces a mesh unsuitable for FEM assembly during training and a topology-change-gradient-free mesh after training. Chen & Zhang 2021's direction — keep topology discrete but amortize the choice — produces an FEM-assembly-compatible mesh but does not buy topology-change gradients in the first place. Both approaches were designed for inverse-rendering and shape-reconstruction tasks that tolerate one or the other trade-off. `sim-soft`'s downstream FEM consumer tolerates neither.

The [DMTet approach](02-diff-delaunay.md) takes a different architectural route: fix the topology at a level where it barely changes, and bury the residual topology events inside rare grid-cell transitions. [§02](02-diff-delaunay.md) treats that line. For the `sim-soft` fallback that accepts the gradient hit, [§03 fd-wrappers](03-fd-wrappers.md) covers the compromise the book actually commits to.

## What this sub-leaf commits the book to

- **Deep Marching Cubes ([Liao, Donné, Geiger 2018](../../appendices/00-references/03-diff-sim.md#liao-2018)) is the soft-topology research line.** Per-corner occupancies + expectation loss + training-dynamics sharpening. The mesh is a distribution during training; phantom vertices and fractional connectivity break FEM assembly on the intermediate state.
- **Neural Marching Cubes ([Chen & Zhang 2021](../../appendices/00-references/03-diff-sim.md#chen-zhang-2021)) is the amortized-discrete research line.** Binary-sign LUT with 37 learned topology cases + smooth vertex offsets. Produces a fully-specified mesh at every forward, but topology-change gradients vanish because the sign classification is itself discrete.
- **Neither is a `sim-soft` drop-in for the design-mode loop.** The `sim-soft` compromise is the [three-part topology-fixed-per-episode treatment](../05-diff-meshing.md) that accepts the Dirac and wraps it in FD at boundary crossings, rather than trying to smooth the topology across all configurations at once.
