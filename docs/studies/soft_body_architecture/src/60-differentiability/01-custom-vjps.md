# Custom VJPs

A vector-Jacobian product (VJP) is the unit of work reverse-mode autograd actually performs: given an upstream gradient $\bar y = \partial L / \partial y$ flowing backward through a node $y = f(x)$, the node's VJP returns $\bar x = J_f^T\, \bar y$ — the gradient pushed one link further back. For elementary ops (add, multiply, tanh) the VJP is a one-liner the autograd library ships with. For FEM assembly and IPC barrier evaluation it is neither. This chapter names the three places `sim-soft` has to write VJPs by hand and the registration machinery that lets the chassis see them.

| [FEM assembly VJP](01-custom-vjps/01-fem-assembly.md) | The per-element stiffness matrix assembly $K = \sum_e B_e^T\, \mathbb{C}_e\, B_e\, V^e$ — why autodiffing through it element-by-element produces a pathologically large tape, and the hand-derived adjoint that replaces it with a single reverse pass over the connectivity |
| [Contact barrier VJP](01-custom-vjps/02-contact-barrier.md) | The IPC barrier $b(d) = -(d - \hat d)^2 \ln(d/\hat d)$ — fragile in two regimes (catastrophic cancellation at $d = \hat d$, Hessian ill-conditioning as $d \to 0^+$); the VJP evaluates the closed-form derivatives directly, per [IPC Toolkit](../appendices/00-references/00-ipc.md#ipc-toolkit), and leaves the barrier-variant choice (clamped log, quadratic log, cubic, two-stage) to a Phase B decision |

Three claims Ch 01 rests on:

1. **Some kernels are adjoints, not graphs.** A pure reverse-mode tape over FEM assembly would record one node per per-element multiply for thousands of elements — the tape allocation and the per-node backward call both dominate the actual arithmetic. Writing the assembly VJP by hand — as one function that walks the connectivity list backward with a gradient accumulator — is both faster per step and stable against the full tape blowing up when the mesh scales. The hand-derived version is the only one that survives into [Phase E's GPU port](../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order).
2. **Numerical stability is not free from automatic differentiation.** The IPC barrier is smooth on paper ($b(d) = -(d-\hat d)^2 \ln(d/\hat d)$, smooth on $(0, \hat d)$ with $b(\hat d) = 0$ and $b(d) \to +\infty$ as $d \to 0^+$) but its double-precision evaluation is fragile in two distinct regimes: catastrophic cancellation at the smooth junction $d = \hat d$ where the factors of $b'(d)$ vanish simultaneously, and Hessian ill-conditioning near $d \to 0^+$ where $b''(d) \propto 1/d^2$ diverges by design. `sim-soft` evaluates the closed-form derivatives the same way the [IPC Toolkit](../appendices/00-references/00-ipc.md#ipc-toolkit) does — direct algebra, no log1p recentering, no Taylor expansion — and leaves the barrier-variant choice (clamped log, normalized, quadratic log, cubic, two-stage) to a Phase B decision based on condition-number measurements. [Li et al. 2020](../appendices/00-references/00-ipc.md#li-2020) introduced the barrier; [GIPC (Huang et al. 2024)](../appendices/00-references/00-ipc.md#gipc-2024) documents the Hessian ill-conditioning and proposes an analytic-eigensystem reformulation `sim-soft` may adopt at the Phase E GPU port.
3. **The registration API is the chassis-level boundary.** `sim-soft` does not fork `sim-ml-chassis`; it extends it with a trait that lets a solver kernel register `(forward, backward)` with the tape. The chassis keeps its ownership of the tape, the scheduling, and the gradient accumulation; `sim-soft` keeps its ownership of the physics-specific adjoints. This is the same composition pattern the chassis uses everywhere else — Algorithm and VecEnv compose by trait, not by fork — and it is what keeps `sim-ml-chassis`'s surface from ballooning every time a new solver domain lands on top of it.
