# The time adjoint method

[Ch 02's IFT](02-implicit-function.md) gives the gradient of a *steady state* w.r.t. design parameters. It does not give the gradient of a *trajectory* — the full $x(t)$ rollout through a [backward-Euler Newton loop](../50-time-integration/00-backward-euler.md) across hundreds to thousands of timesteps. For trajectory-level objectives — integrated reward over a squeeze-and-release cycle, energy-dissipation quality, end-effector-path tracking — the gradient requires the *time adjoint*: a backward-in-time integration of an auxiliary variable $\lambda(t)$ that carries the objective's sensitivity from the terminal state back to the parameters. This chapter names what the adjoint delivers, contrasts it against forward sensitivity, and points to [Ch 04 checkpointing](04-checkpointing.md) for the memory problem the adjoint creates.

| Section | What it covers |
|---|---|
| [Forward vs reverse through time](03-time-adjoint/00-forward-reverse.md) | Forward sensitivity integrates $\partial x / \partial \theta$ forward alongside $x$ — cost is $O(n_\theta)$ per step, fine for $n_\theta \lesssim 10$; reverse adjoint integrates $\lambda$ *backward* — cost is $O(1)$ per step regardless of $n_\theta$, but requires forward trajectory access for the adjoint ODE's coefficients |
| [Adjoint state equation](03-time-adjoint/01-adjoint-state.md) | The continuous-time adjoint $\dot\lambda = -(\partial f / \partial x)^T\, \lambda - (\partial g / \partial x)^T$ with terminal condition $\lambda(T) = (\partial \Phi / \partial x)(x(T))$; its discrete-time analogue for backward-Euler; interplay with [per-step IFT](02-implicit-function.md) since every forward step is itself an implicit solve |
| [Stochastic adjoints](03-time-adjoint/02-stochastic.md) | When the forward trajectory contains stochastic terms — Brownian forcing for thermo-fluctuation coupling, policy-injected action noise — the adjoint ODE becomes a backward SDE; the Stratonovich vs Itô choice and the resulting estimator variance |

Three claims Ch 03 rests on:

1. **Reverse wins past a few parameters.** For a design space of 10 scalar parameters or fewer, forward sensitivity is the right choice: it is simpler to implement, avoids the need to store the forward trajectory, and maps directly onto existing forward-mode AD. For a material field with thousands of SDF-valued stiffness coefficients ([Part 2 Ch 09](../20-materials/09-spatial-fields.md)) or a policy network with $10^5$ weights feeding into [the full design-print-rate loop](../100-optimization/06-full-loop.md), reverse adjoint is the only affordable path. The break-even on `sim-soft` is around $n_\theta \approx 20$; above that, reverse is uniformly cheaper.

2. **The adjoint couples to the per-step IFT.** A backward-Euler step from $x_{t-1}$ to $x_t$ is itself an implicit solve: $x_t$ satisfies $r(x_t, x_{t-1}; \theta) = 0$ where $r$ encodes the residual of the energy-minimization problem at step $t$. The discrete-time adjoint therefore threads through *the same factorizations* the forward solve computed at each step — one back-substitution per backward step, each with the Jacobian at that step's converged state. The memory cost is the factorizations along the forward trajectory; [Ch 04](04-checkpointing.md) names the Revolve algorithm as the way to recover a linear-in-$T$ memory footprint at the price of $\log T$ extra forward re-solves.

3. **Stochastic adjoints are required for the thermo-RL loop.** [`sim-thermostat`](../110-crate/02-coupling/01-thermostat.md)'s dissipative-heating coupling includes Brownian forcing when modeling microscale thermal fluctuations; the [custom thermo-RL project](../10-physical/00-canonical.md) relies on that noise as part of its exploration strategy. The adjoint of an SDE is a backward SDE driven by a time-reversed Brownian motion ([Li et al. 2020 SDE adjoint](../appendices/00-references/02-adjoint.md#li-2020-sde), [Kidger et al. 2021](../appendices/00-references/02-adjoint.md#kidger-2021)); the resulting estimator is unbiased but higher variance than the deterministic adjoint. `sim-soft` defers the stochastic-adjoint implementation to [Phase H](../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order) with the rest of the viscoelastic/thermal upgrades — the deterministic adjoint ships in Phase D and covers every objective that does not cross a noise boundary.
