# Hyperelastic curve fits

Dragon Skin's stretch window on the canonical problem is narrower than Ecoflex's — 10A and 20A run mostly at moderate stretch ($\lambda \in [1, 2]$), 30A sees a stiffer-small-stretch regime still — and this shifts which hyperelastic form is the best working fit across the ladder. Where [the Ecoflex hyperelastic-fits leaf](../00-ecoflex/01-hyperelastic-fits.md) settled on Ogden $N = 3$ as the canonical-regime default, Dragon Skin sits in [Mooney-Rivlin](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md)'s stated sweet spot per [Part 2 Ch 04 — Mooney-Rivlin Claim 3](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md). The fit-selection story for this family therefore centres on the moderate-stretch regime, with Ogden reserved for the 30A-under-large-squeeze boundary case the [Dragon Skin branch](../01-dragonskin.md) Claim 4 flags.

## Uniaxial forms

The scalar uniaxial Cauchy-stress expressions under incompressibility are the same ones the [Ecoflex hyperelastic-fits leaf](../00-ecoflex/01-hyperelastic-fits.md) names — $\sigma_{\text{NH}}(\lambda) = \mu(\lambda^2 - \lambda^{-1})$, $\sigma_{\text{MR}}(\lambda) = 2(\lambda^2 - \lambda^{-1})(C_{10} + C_{01}\lambda^{-1})$, and the principal-stretch Ogden sum — and the tensorial forms live in [Part 2 Ch 04](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic.md). What changes for Dragon Skin is the regime where each form holds and the published data available to fit each.

## Literature coverage

Dragon Skin is substantially less characterized in the soft-robotics and silicone-elastomer literature than Ecoflex. Most academic papers that report hyperelastic fits of Smooth-On silicones default to Ecoflex grades for their measurement target, leaving Dragon Skin covered by a handful of studies with narrower stretch windows and fewer published coefficient sets. The [hyperelasticity reference leaf](../../../appendices/00-references/01-hyperelastic.md) reserves a Pass-3 anchor for the Dragon Skin curve-fit study the book commits to once the source-check settles which published fit is the working reference. Until then the book's working position is:

- **Dragon Skin 10A** — neo-Hookean $\mu$ from the Shore-A correlation ([mechanical-data sibling leaf](00-mechanical.md)) is the always-available fit; Mooney-Rivlin coefficients from the [calibration sub-chapter's](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin/01-calibration.md) mixed uniaxial-plus-biaxial protocol are the Pass-3 measurement deliverable.
- **Dragon Skin 20A** — same status as 10A. The Shore-A correlation is at its tightest here.
- **Dragon Skin 30A** — same status, with a reminder from the [branch](../01-dragonskin.md) that this grade can enter the large-squeeze regime where the Mooney-Rivlin form saturates and Ogden is needed.

The literature-coverage gap is why the [Part 10 Ch 05 sim-to-real loop](../../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) carries more weight for Dragon Skin than for Ecoflex: per-print calibration substitutes for the published-fit density Ecoflex enjoys.

## Fit-form selection across the ladder

**Neo-Hookean.** At the default single-parameter calibration — $\mu$ fit to the measured curve across the canonical-problem stretch range — neo-Hookean tracks Dragon Skin 10A and 20A under moderate stretch with the same few-percent fit quality the [Part 2 Ch 04 neo-Hookean branch Claim 1](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/00-neo-hookean.md) commits to for silicones under their breakdown stretch. This is the material-database-anchor fit: the appendix table's always-present neo-Hookean $\mu$ row is sufficient for the moderate-stretch regime these two grades sit in. For 30A under small stretch, neo-Hookean remains accurate; for 30A approaching the 364% break elongation, the $\lambda^2$ over-prediction re-emerges and the fit is no longer the working choice.

**Mooney-Rivlin.** The two-parameter form is Dragon Skin's canonical working fit. The [Mooney-Rivlin branch's Claim 3](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md) names the moderate-stretch regime — "stiff enough that the $I_1$-only fit begins to drift, not so stiff that the $N{=}3$ Ogden form is needed" — as its operating window, and Dragon Skin 10A and 20A under cyclic loading sit cleanly in that window. Calibration needs more than a uniaxial curve for the reasons the [calibration sub-chapter](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin/01-calibration.md) lays out: the $(C_{10}, C_{01})$ pair is under-determined by uniaxial data alone, and a biaxial or planar-shear leg is required. Per-batch sim-to-real calibration closes the shortfall when only uniaxial data is available by anchoring one parameter against a measured equibiaxial test point.

**Ogden $N = 2$ or $N = 3$.** Reserved for Dragon Skin 30A under large-squeeze loading where the Mooney-Rivlin form saturates. The [Ogden branch's Claim 1](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/02-ogden.md) on why principal-stretch forms capture shapes invariant-based forms cannot applies here as well as to Ecoflex, and the branch's Claim 2 on $N = 2$ or $N = 3$ as the practical range applies in the same way. For 30A the book ships both Mooney-Rivlin and Ogden $N = 2$ fits as the boundary-case response — which form the solver uses at a given Gauss point is a `Material`-selector decision made from the local stretch magnitude at `MaterialField` construction time, per the [canonical-problem design flow](../../../10-physical/00-canonical.md).

## The boundary case — 30A under large squeeze

Dragon Skin 30A is the one grade where the fit-form selection is genuinely regime-dependent within the canonical problem's operating envelope. Under small-displacement cavity-conformity with a mild probe, 30A runs cleanly in Mooney-Rivlin territory. Under a deep insertion or a high-cycle squeeze where stretches approach the break elongation of 364%, the Mooney-Rivlin saturation the [Mooney-Rivlin branch Claim 3](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md) names takes over, and Ogden is the only fit-form that tracks the curve to the edge of validity.

The `Material` trait's validity-domain declaration handles this the same way the rest of the book handles regime-dependent fit quality: the `Material<MooneyRivlin>` implementation declares its validity as a stretch bound, and when a Gauss point exceeds that bound the gradcheck suite emits a runtime warning and the designer can opt into an `Material<Ogden<2>>` override for that region via the [spatial-fields machinery](../../../20-materials/09-spatial-fields.md). The default is Mooney-Rivlin; the Ogden upgrade is opt-in, not automatic, because the extra parameters require a calibration dataset the default Dragon Skin 30A row does not ship with.

## What the sim-soft `Material` stores

The `Material<MooneyRivlin>` implementation for Dragon Skin holds the $(C_{10}, C_{01})$ pair plus the validity-metadata stretch bound. The `MaterialField` [from Part 7 Ch 00](../../../70-sdf-pipeline/00-sdf-primitive.md) samples these per element; the [Part 2 Ch 09 spatial-field machinery](../../../20-materials/09-spatial-fields.md) interpolates the shear-modulus-related combination $C_{10} + C_{01}$ directly when a `MaterialField<MooneyRivlin>` varies across space, with the individual $C_{10}$ and $C_{01}$ transferring via interpolation at the canonical-problem scale.

For multi-material scenes combining Dragon Skin and Ecoflex regions (the compliant-cavity-with-a-stiff-core geometry the [Dragon Skin branch](../01-dragonskin.md) Claim 1 highlights), the `MaterialField` crosses the family boundary through a `Material<NeoHookean>`-to-`Material<MooneyRivlin>`-to-`Material<Ogden>` transition surface, which `sim-soft` handles as a smooth blend in the small-strain shear modulus $\mu$ with per-region parameters switching at the validity-domain boundary.

## Alternatives considered

**Ship Ogden $N = 3$ as the default for all three Dragon Skin grades.** Rejected because Dragon Skin's operating stretch window on the canonical problem is narrower than Ecoflex's, and the extra four Ogden parameters buy accuracy the canonical-problem regime does not spend. The [Mooney-Rivlin branch Claim 3](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md) "narrow and specific" operating window is Dragon Skin's normal operating window, not an edge case.

**Ship only neo-Hookean for all three grades.** Rejected because the moderate-stretch regime past 50% stretch is exactly where the $I_1$-only form starts to drift, per the same [Mooney-Rivlin branch Claim 3](../../../20-materials/04-hyperelastic/01-mooney-rivlin.md). Paying the one-extra-invariant cost of Mooney-Rivlin is what the form exists for; a neo-Hookean-only Dragon Skin appendix would be a false economy.

**Defer Mooney-Rivlin calibration to sim-to-real entirely and ship neo-Hookean rows.** Rejected as the default. Deferring all multi-parameter calibration to sim-to-real strips the appendix rows of the working fits the [Part 1 Ch 04 material-data parent](../../04-material-data.md)'s "real measured data, not textbook placeholders" claim rests on. Pass-3 measurement of the $(C_{10}, C_{01})$ pair for each Dragon Skin grade is on the roadmap; until then the appendix row ships a neo-Hookean $\mu$ value with a Mooney-Rivlin Pass-3 slot marked explicitly.
