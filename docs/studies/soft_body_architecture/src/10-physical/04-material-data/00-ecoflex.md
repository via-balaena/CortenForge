# Ecoflex family

Ecoflex is Smooth-On's platinum-cure silicone line at the soft end of the Shore hardness scale. `sim-soft` uses it as the workhorse soft material in the canonical problem, per [Part 1 Ch 04 — material data](../04-material-data.md), because four things line up: the grade ladder covers roughly a 4× small-strain stiffness range in a single chemistry, the cure works at room temperature without exotic equipment, the soft-robotics characterization literature includes hyperelastic curve fits through most of the useful stretch range for the mid grades, and the Shore 00 durometer sits where soft-robotics conformity problems actually live. The four sub-chapters below split the family's data story into its pieces; the [appendices material database](../../appendices/02-material-db.md) carries the specific nominal values each sub-chapter refers back to, so this branch stays narrative rather than re-tabling numbers.

Four claims the Ecoflex branch rests on:

1. **The four-grade ladder is the reason `sim-soft` picks this family.** Ecoflex 00-10 at the soft end and 00-50 at the stiff end span roughly a 4× small-strain modulus range with consistent chemistry and overlapping processing (cure time, mix ratio, pot life). A designer optimizing conformity-versus-stability on the canonical problem can walk the ladder without switching material chemistries — the only thing that changes between grades is the durometer, and [Part 10 Ch 02 BayesOpt](../../100-optimization/02-bayesopt.md) sees a single continuous stiffness parameter rather than a material-family switch. This is what makes the spatial-material-field story of [Part 2 Ch 09 — spatial fields](../../20-materials/09-spatial-fields.md) practical: smooth $\mu(x)$ fields over a single elastomer family, not heterogeneous chemistry with discontinuous processing.

2. **Published characterization covers the stretch range the canonical problem needs.** The [hyperelastic curve fits sub-chapter](00-ecoflex/01-hyperelastic-fits.md) names the specific published uniaxial stress–strain studies — anchored at Pass 3 in [the hyperelasticity reference leaf](../../appendices/00-references/01-hyperelastic.md) — that characterize 00-30 and 00-50 through moderate-to-large stretch. 00-10 and 00-20 are less commonly characterized in the literature, and the [material database](../../appendices/02-material-db.md) rows for those grades are flagged as Shore-correlation-derived with uniaxial measurement scheduled as a Pass-3 deliverable. Every Ecoflex value that reaches the solver is either cited or explicitly scheduled for measurement — the no-textbook-placeholders claim from [Part 1 Ch 04](../04-material-data.md).

3. **The viscoelastic spectrum is modest but load-bearing.** Silicone dissipation is smaller than natural rubber's by a wide margin, and a quasi-static simulation can appear to get away without a viscoelastic term. It does not. A Prony-series fit from the [Part 2 Ch 07 DMA protocol](../../20-materials/07-viscoelastic/03-dma.md) is what closes the [Ch 02 wobble failure](../02-what-goes-wrong/01-wobble.md) — the measured spectrum does not vary dramatically across the Ecoflex grade ladder, so a single $N = 3$ Prony fit calibrated on 00-30 transfers reasonably to the other three grades at this pass's fidelity, matching the "$N = 3$ or $4$ for silicones" convention of [Part 2 Ch 07](../../20-materials/07-viscoelastic.md). Per-grade refinement and the $N = 4$ upgrade are Pass-3 deliverables alongside the Ogden coefficient sets.

4. **Ecoflex's thermal envelope is narrow and predictable.** Thermal expansion at $\alpha \approx 3 \times 10^{-4}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}$ is roughly uniform across the grade ladder, and thermal conductivity sits in a narrow band below 0.2 W/m·K — low enough that dissipative heating can accumulate over sustained cyclic loading before it conducts away, which is the coupling loop [`sim-thermostat`](../../20-materials/08-thermal-coupling.md) closes. These values are data-sheet-derived rather than measured in-house, and lot-to-lot variance is below what matters for the canonical problem's short transient runs; the [Part 10 Ch 05 sim-to-real loop](../../100-optimization/05-sim-to-real.md) remains the final calibration authority for extended-load experiments where drift matters.
