# Coupling with rigid-body timestep

`sim-soft` simulates deformable bodies; `sim-core` runs the rigid-body solver (a MuJoCo-pipeline-aligned pure-Rust implementation, populated from MJCF models by `sim-mjcf`). The [canonical problem](../10-physical/00-canonical.md) has both: a compliant cavity ([sim-soft](../110-crate/00-module-layout.md)) contacted by a rigid probe ([sim-core](../110-crate/02-coupling/00-mjcf.md), defined in an MJCF file via `sim-mjcf`). The two simulators have different timesteps, different solver families, and different conventions for representing contact. This chapter names the co-simulation scheme that bridges them and explains why a monolithic "one timestep solves both" approach was rejected.

Note on the crate triad. `sim-mjcf` is the MJCF (MuJoCo XML Format) loader — it parses `.xml` model files and populates `sim-core`'s `Model` / `Data` structures. `sim-core` is the rigid-body runtime that advances `Data` through time using the MuJoCo algorithmic pipeline (re-implemented in Rust, not a wrapper around the MuJoCo C++ library). MuJoCo itself is the external reference — the conventions and algorithms `sim-core` follows. Downstream of Ch 03 the book uses `sim-core` when the runtime matters and `sim-mjcf` only when the file-format or load path does.

Two claims this chapter rests on.

**Fixed-point co-simulation, not monolithic assembly.** A monolithic scheme would assemble one Hessian containing both rigid-body and deformable DOFs, factor it once per Newton step, and solve the whole system jointly. The closest academic analogues are constraint-coupled approaches — FEBio's augmented-Lagrangian enforcement of rigid–deformable constraints ([Maas et al. 2012](../appendices/00-references/06-co-simulation.md#febio-2012)), SOFA's mappings between rigid and deformable DOF subspaces — rather than single-Hessian assembly proper, which is uncommon in production academic codes. For `sim-soft`, the monolithic route would require either absorbing `sim-core`'s pipeline (rewriting it internally, which is off-platform) or reimplementing rigid-body dynamics (redundant with `sim-core`, the [rigid-body foundation-of-record](../110-crate/02-coupling/00-mjcf.md)). The fixed-point alternative treats each simulator as a black box: advance `sim-soft` one step with rigid-body boundary conditions held fixed, advance `sim-core` through the corresponding interval with contact-force boundary conditions held fixed, exchange the new boundary data, and repeat until the iterate-to-iterate boundary-data change falls below tolerance. Each simulator retains its own native solver; the coupling is an outer-loop handshake. The [fixed-point sub-chapter](03-coupling/01-fixed-point.md) covers the convergence criterion and typical iteration counts; partitioned analysis as a framework traces to [Felippa and Park 1980](../appendices/00-references/06-co-simulation.md#felippa-park-1980), fixed-point coupling with differing sub-domain timesteps to [Gravouil and Combescure 2001](../appendices/00-references/06-co-simulation.md#gravouil-combescure-2001).

**The native timesteps differ.** `sim-core` defaults to MuJoCo's 2 ms rigid-body timestep ([official MJCF XML reference](https://mujoco.readthedocs.io/en/stable/XMLreference.html) — `<option timestep="0.002">`); contact-heavy scenes in practice often reduce this toward 0.5–1 ms (practitioner heuristic across the MuJoCo community — the official guidance is to reduce from the default when contact becomes unstable, without a single canonical range). `sim-soft` runs a soft-body timestep of 16 ms in experience mode or 1 ms in design mode. The fixed-point coupling resolves the mismatch by treating the coarser simulator's step as the handshake period: within one `sim-soft` step of 16 ms, `sim-core` advances 8–32 consecutive rigid-body steps between handshakes (8 at the 2 ms default, 32 at 0.5 ms). The coupling exchange happens at the `sim-soft` rate. The [Phase F deliverable](../110-crate/03-build-order.md#the-committed-order) is this handshake working end-to-end on the canonical problem; details of the step-count mismatch and the iteration-convergence criterion are in the [fixed-point sub-chapter](03-coupling/01-fixed-point.md).

## Boundary data crossing the interface

Four fields cross the `sim-soft` ↔ `sim-core` boundary at each handshake.

- **Rigid pose + velocity** (`sim-core` → `sim-soft`): the probe's current $SE(3)$ pose and spatial velocity, used by `sim-soft`'s contact detection to find contact pairs between deformable-body surface tets and rigid-body geometry.
- **Contact force + contact-point locations** (`sim-soft` → `sim-core`): the contact pressure field integrated over contact area, applied as an external wrench on the rigid body via the MuJoCo-convention `xfrc_applied` per-body array in `sim-core`'s `Data`; the MuJoCo pipeline projects `xfrc_applied` through the body Jacobian into generalized force during `mj_fwdAcceleration`.
- **Pin constraint reactions** (optional, `sim-soft` → `sim-core`): if the deformable body is pinned to a rigid body (e.g., a silicone sleeve bonded to a rigid mount), the constraint reaction force on the pin is fed back.
- **Temperature and heat flux** (optional, via [`sim-thermostat`](../110-crate/02-coupling/01-thermostat.md)): if thermal coupling is enabled, the deformable-body temperature field at the rigid-contact interface.

Each field has a canonical representation documented in `sim-soft::coupling::mjcf` (see [Part 11 Ch 02 sub-chapter 00](../110-crate/02-coupling/00-mjcf.md)); the [Part 5 Ch 03 sub-chapter 00](03-coupling/00-mujoco.md) covers the co-simulation API and serialization details.

## What this does NOT cover

- **Coupling with `sim-thermostat`** — the temperature-field handshake is architecturally similar but lives in [Part 2 Ch 08](../20-materials/08-thermal-coupling.md) and [Part 11 Ch 02 sub-chapter 01](../110-crate/02-coupling/01-thermostat.md), since the physical coupling (dissipative heating, temperature-dependent modulus) is material-layer concern rather than time-integration concern.
- **Coupling with `cf-design`** — design-parameter edits are handled at the [SDF bridge](../70-sdf-pipeline/00-sdf-primitive.md) layer, not the time-integration layer. An SDF edit triggers re-meshing and warm-started re-solve; it does not cross through the `sim-core` handshake.
- **Multi-soft-body coupling** — multiple `sim-soft` instances coupled through contact. Not a Phase F concern and not on the [Phase A–I roadmap](../110-crate/03-build-order.md); when it lands, it will use the same fixed-point scheme between instances.
