## Generic Smooth-On Guidance

- Weigh Part A + Part B on a gram scale. 1A:1B is by weight or volume per TDS — choose the more accurate path for the equipment on hand (weight typically beats volume for low-viscosity silicones).
- Vacuum-degas the mixed silicone at ≥27 inHg for 2-3 minutes before pouring. Bubbles trapped at cavity surfaces or layer interfaces are the #1 first-cast failure mode.
- Apply mold release to printed surfaces before each pour. Layer-to-layer adhesion between cured silicones is generally strong without release, but printed-mold-to-silicone separation needs it.
- Pour into the deepest point of the cavity and let the silicone self-level outward; pouring across surfaces traps air.
- Cure at room temperature (≈23 °C). Cooler slows cure; warmer accelerates. Heat-cure schedules can shorten demold time per TDS but introduce thermal-shrink effects not modeled here.

## Per-Layer Procedure

### Layer 0 — Ecoflex 00-30 (innermost)

1. Print STL `mold_layer_0.stl` and the shared `plug.stl`.
2. Apply mold release to all printed surfaces.
3. Mix 21.01 g Part A + 21.01 g Part B Ecoflex 00-30 (1A:1B mix ratio). Total 42.01 g.
4. Vacuum-degas the mix for 2-3 minutes at ≥27 inHg.
5. Pour into the mold cup. Insert the plug from +z, displacing silicone to shape the inner cavity.
6. Cure for ≥4 hr at 73 °F (pot life: 45 min).
7. Demold from the mold cup and carefully extract the plug.

### Layer 1 — Dragon Skin 10A (middle)
