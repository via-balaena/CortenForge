## 0.5. Pivot — BCC + Isosurface Stuffing replaces uniform cubic + MT-clip (2026-04-27)

**What was tried.** Commits 1–5 of this branch shipped a uniform-cubic-lattice + 6-tet Kuhn decomposition + Marching-Tetrahedra clipping + degenerate-tet filter as the placeholder mesher per the original §3 Decision A. Commit 6's first run of the III-2 quality-bounds sweep (9 combinations: `r ∈ {0.05, 0.1, 0.2}` × `cell_size ∈ {r/3, r/5, r/8}`) revealed that all three sanity-floor invariants fail across all 9 combinations. Worst observed extrema:

| Floor | Memo-locked bound | Worst observed | Margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| `ρ_min` (aspect ratio) | `≥ 0.05` | `0.0001` | 500× below |
| `α_lo` (dihedral min) | `≥ 5°` | `0.16°` | 31× below |
| `α_hi` (dihedral max) | `≤ 175°` | `178.22°` | 3.22° over |

Aspect / dihedral are scale-invariant — the same extrema repeat across `r` at fixed cells-per-radius — so the per-tet quality kernel is correct; the algorithm is the problem. Full 9-row sweep table preserved in Appendix A.

**Why it fails.** S-8 lens (vi) was prophetic: MT-clip cuts each Kuhn-tet edge at `t = -v0 / (v1 - v0)`. When a sphere zero-crossing lands near (but not AT) a grid corner, `t` is near 0 or 1 and the cut produces a sub-tet with a near-degenerate edge — a sliver, not a `vol < EPSILON_VOLUME` degenerate that the D-10 filter would catch. Worst observed `vol_min ≈ 1.9e-14 m³` is one order of magnitude above `EPSILON_VOLUME = 1e-15 m³` — the D-10 filter is sized correctly and is dropping the truly-zero-volume tail; the sliver tail at `vol ~ 1e-14` and above survives. The pathology is structural to *uniform cubic + linear edge interpolation*, not a parameter-tuning issue.

**What was chosen — BCC + Labelle-Shewchuk Isosurface Stuffing (SIGGRAPH 2007).** A body-centered cubic lattice (BCC = simple cubic + one extra vertex per cube center) plus the warp + stuffing pipeline from the Labelle-Shewchuk paper. BCC eliminates the near-corner pathology by construction:

1. **Warp step:** for any lattice vertex `v` whose SDF value `|f(v)|` is below a tabulated `α · ‖edge‖` threshold for some incident edge crossing zero, `v` is *snapped* directly to the zero-crossing (vertex displacement, not edge cut). Sub-tets sharing `v` then share the snapped position by construction; no near-vertex slivers can be emitted.
2. **Stuffing case table:** classify each BCC tet's 4 vertex signs (post-warp), look up the case in the stuffing table, emit 0–3 sub-tets per case. The case table is small (16 raw cases reducing to 5 unique up to BCC's symmetries).
3. **Output guarantee:** Labelle-Shewchuk's published quality theorem guarantees output dihedral angles bounded inside a fixed interval and aspect ratios bounded below a fixed positive constant. The basic version of the algorithm is published with bounds approximately `[10.7°, 164.8°]` on dihedral angles (exact theorem number to be cited at commit-5 from the paper); refinements named in the paper tighten further. The α tabulation values (`α_long`, `α_short`) governing the warp threshold are pinned at commit-5 by direct citation from the paper's table — the values sketched elsewhere in this memo (`≈ 0.21`, `≈ 0.42`) are reference-from-memory placeholders subject to that verification. **Bottom line for the sanity floor:** the published Labelle-Shewchuk bounds clear `α_lo ≥ 5°`, `α_hi ≤ 175°` with substantial margin under either the basic or the refined α values; the III-2 floor is cleared by *theorem*, not an empirical ask. If commit-5 verification finds the published bounds DON'T clear the floor (extremely unlikely given the paper's reputation), surface as material plan change rather than relax thresholds.

**Why this beats the alternatives weighed at the abort signal.** Three other options were considered and rejected (full case-by-case in Appendix A):

- *Snap-to-vertex tolerance bolted onto MT-clip* — papers over the symptom; placeholder ceiling stays placeholder; no foundation reuse for Phase H.
- *Relax the sanity floor* — purely defers failure to III-3 (sliver Hessian conditioning eats Decision O's 5-digit FD slack); defeats the floor's purpose.
- *Percentile invariant (e.g., 95% above floor)* — actively reduces the gate's ceiling; future regressions slip through; conflicts with `feedback_fix_gaps_before_continuing`.

**Why BCC's ceiling is uniquely high (the load-bearing reason).**

- *Quality is a theorem, not a hope.* Floor compliance is proved, not measured.
- *Algorithmic family consistent with quality-bounded meshers.* Phase H's eventual mesh-quality target (whether fTetWild proper or a similar envelope-driven approach) lives in the same theorem-bounded-quality regime as BCC + stuffing; the Phase 3 BCC lattice substrate is reusable as a starting tessellation that envelope-driven refinement can act on. Even if Phase H's algorithm doesn't *literally* take BCC as input, the foundation work — case-table reasoning, warp-step determinism, edge-cache encoding under a bipartite lattice — translates. A snap-to-MT placeholder offers none of that; the hack would be entirely discarded at Phase H.
- *Multi-material aligns naturally.* The Phase-4 layered-silicone target needs sharp inter-material interfaces; BCC labels lattice vertices per material and stuffs each region independently with shared warped interfaces. MT-on-cubic forces every interface through the same near-corner pathology and re-amplifies it at every material boundary.
- *Adaptive refinement maps cleanly.* Octree-refined BCC is the standard foundation for Phase G interactive re-mesh and Phase H's adaptive pass.
- *Well-cited and well-documented.* Labelle-Shewchuk 2007 is one of the most-cited tetrahedral-meshing papers in graphics; the case table fits in ~250 LOC; risk-of-getting-wrong is bounded by the published reference.

**Cost.** Phase 3 grows from 10 to ~12 commits; ~300 LOC heavier than the pre-pivot placeholder. Branch surgery: commits 4–5 (`f8e0a1d9`, `4b3a189c`) get reset off the branch; commits 1–3 (sdf_bridge scaffolding, quality kernel, QualityMetrics field grow — all algorithm-agnostic) stay. The pre-pivot tip is preserved as a git tag (`feature/phase-3-sdf-tet-bridge-pre-pivot-bcc`) before the reset for audit. Pre-squash tag (Decision D) still cuts on the post-pivot branch tip immediately before squash-merge.

**Decisions affected by the pivot.** Decision A (algorithm) replaced. Decision M (determinism + correctness mitigations) revised — D-8 stays, D-9 changes shape (BCC edge cache keys differ), D-10 demoted from load-bearing to defensive backstop (BCC + stuffing produces no zero-volume sub-tets by theorem; the filter exists only for FP-edge-case insurance), D-11 added for warp-step determinism. Decision N (BF-10 absorption) text adjusted to mention BCC. Decisions B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, J, K, L, O, P unchanged. §1 invariants III-1/III-2/III-3 unchanged in contract; algorithm-specific phrasing inside each refreshed. §6 stress-rounds S-1/S-2/S-3/S-8(vi) refreshed. §8 sequencing restructured — commits 4–5 split into three new commits.

**Future re-pivot triggers.** If BCC-stuffing implementation surfaces (a) unforeseen quality bound below the sanity floor *despite* the warp theorem (would indicate either a case-table bug or a Labelle-Shewchuk implementation deviation), (b) determinism failures that the warp + edge-cache keying can't accommodate, or (c) a >50% scope blowout vs. this revised target (~1,500 LOC ceiling), surface as another material plan change. Expected outcomes: none — Labelle-Shewchuk's algorithm is well-specified and the foundation is sound; surfacing exists only because the abort path here was real and the same discipline must apply going forward.

## 1. Load-bearing invariants

Three new invariants gate Phase 3; one optional. Each is a code-level check that one specific SDF-bridge claim holds.

| # | Invariant | What green looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **III-1** | **SDF→tet pipeline determinism.** | Two consecutive runs of `SdfMeshedTetMesh::from_sdf(&SphereSdf { radius: 0.1 }, &MeshingHints { bbox: Aabb3::new(Vec3::new(-0.12, -0.12, -0.12), Vec3::new(0.12, 0.12, 0.12)), cell_size: 0.02 })` (bbox just contains the sphere; outermost cells are boundary cells with the sphere surface passing through their interior, handled by the warp + stuffing pipeline without near-vertex sliver pathology) produce mesh-equal output: same `n_vertices`, same `n_tets`, bit-equal positions (`f64::to_bits`), bit-equal `tet_vertices` per tet ID, bit-equal `QualityMetrics` fields per tet ID. | **III-2** | **Quality bounds on parametric sphere family.** | For `r ∈ {0.05, 0.1, 0.2}` m and `cell_size ∈ {r/3, r/5, r/8}`: every output mesh satisfies (a) every tet has `signed_volume > 0` (right-handed by warp + stuffing construction), (b) every tet has `aspect_ratio ≥ ρ_min`, (c) every tet has `dihedral_min ≥ α_lo` and `dihedral_max ≤ α_hi`, (d) volume monotonicity: `vol(r=0.05) < vol(r=0.1) < vol(r=0.2)` strictly at fixed `cell_size`. III-2's strict `signed_volume > 0` is the detector. Specific empirical thresholds within the floor are commit-7 work (post-pivot sequencing); Phase H tightens further with envelope-driven refinement. |
| **III-3** | **ForwardMap survives mesher output, end-to-end.** | An `SdfMeshedTetMesh` constructed from `SphereSdf { radius: 0.1 }` with `cell_size=0.02` and BCs **pinned set = `pick_vertices_by_predicate(&mesh, |p| p.z < 0.0)`** (the entire bottom hemisphere — fully constrains all 6 rigid-body modes since the bottom hemisphere contains many non-collinear vertices) + **load-vertex = single top vertex** (max-z over `mesh.referenced_vertices()` filtered by `p.z > 0.0`) drives a `CpuTet4NHSolver` Newton solve under Stage-1 `+ẑ` traction θ; produces a `RewardBreakdown` where **`peak_bound` and `stiffness_bound` are finite** (the on-tape composition path); **`pressure_uniformity` and `coverage` are `NaN` per the Phase 2 NaN-sentinel contract** preserved at Phase 2 commit 9 — these fields are not on the tape; `score_with(&weights)` returns a finite scalar reward via the NaN-skip path. Under BCC + warp + stuffing the algorithm produces no orphans by construction (warp displaces in-place; no coincident-cut VertexId is created). `mesh::referenced_vertices` is preserved as defense-in-depth for general spatial-predicate BCs but is no longer the load-bearing detector for III-3. **Pin-set sizing rationale:** the bottom-hemisphere predicate pins ~half the mesh vertices; this is conservative (more pins than strictly necessary for rigid-body constraint, ≥3 non-collinear pins suffice) but matches walking-skeleton's "majority-pin, single-load" pattern (1-tet had 3 of 4 vertices pinned) and ensures well-conditioned Hessian for the larger DOF count. A tighter predicate (e.g., `p.z < -0.05` pinning only the bottom 25%) would also work but offers thinner conditioning margin. **Scope clarification:** III-3 validates that **Phase 2's IFT machinery survives the BCC mesher's output quality** — Newton converges within budget on the ~3,000-tet body, the sparse Hessian factors, autograd gives correct gradients on the mesher-meshed body. Topology-crossing FD wrapper (which would test `∂(reward)/∂r` for a perturbed radius) is **out of Phase 3 scope** (Decision L). The novel risk Phase 3 exposes: mesher output could be quality-degraded enough that Newton fails to converge or the Hessian factor fails — III-2's quality bounds (cleared by theorem) are the upstream filter; III-3 is the downstream confirmation. |
| III-4 *(optional)* | **Sphere volume convergence to `(4/3)πr³` as `cell_size → 0`.** | At three resolutions (`cell_size = r/3, r/5, r/8`), the relative volume error decreases monotonically (target soft-bound: `≤ ~15%` at `cell_size = r/8` for the placeholder algorithm, NOT load-bearing — empirical observation pinned at commit-7 run-time, post-pivot sequencing). Recommend-first per `feedback_recommend_first_deep_specialist`; not blocking. The convergence rate question (O(h²) or better) is a Phase H tightening concern; Phase 3 only confirms the qualitative direction. |

**Why these three together are the gate:** III-1 isolates *mesher determinism* (the SDF→tet pipeline is reproducible). III-2 isolates *mesher quality* (the output is geometrically valid + clears the sanity floor; under correct BCC + warp + stuffing operation, the floor is cleared by Labelle-Shewchuk's published quality theorem). III-3 isolates *integration* (the mesher's output is useable downstream by Phase 2's trusted multi-element machinery, including at the new larger DOF scale, with a fully-constrained pin set). III-1 and III-2 must pass before III-3 is attempted; III-3's failure mode otherwise can't be disentangled from mesher bugs.

The Phase 2 invariants II-1, II-2, II-3 stay implicitly active — Phase 3 must not regress any of them. The walking-skeleton invariants I-1 through I-6 and Phase 2's full test suite stay green.

**Decision A (revised 2026-04-27): Algorithm — BCC lattice + Labelle-Shewchuk Isosurface Stuffing (warp + stuffing case table). fTetWild deferred via BF-10.**
User adjudicated 2026-04-27 per the III-2 sanity-floor failure (see §0.5 + Appendix A). The pre-pivot choice (uniform cubic + 6-tet Kuhn + MT-clip + degenerate-tet filter) was abandoned because MT-clip on a non-grid-aligned SDF surface produces sliver tets at near-vertex zero-crossings that violate the memo-locked sanity floor across all 9 III-2 sphere combinations. BCC + Isosurface Stuffing (Labelle & Shewchuk, *SIGGRAPH 2007*) eliminates the near-vertex sliver pathology by construction:

1. **BCC lattice.** Body-centered cubic = simple cubic + one extra vertex per cube center. Lattice vertices at `(i, j, k) · a` (even sublattice) plus `(i+½, j+½, k+½) · a` (odd sublattice) for integer `(i, j, k)` and lattice spacing `a`. The lattice tessellates into all-similar tetrahedra (one congruence class up to rigid motion). The canonical BCC tessellation uses each cube's center vertex as a shared apex of multiple tets that fan out to triples of cube-corner vertices on the cube's faces; the exact tessellation choice (and per-cube tet count, ~12 tets unique-by-ownership when each tet's center-vertex membership uniquely binds it to one cube) is pinned at commit-4 implementation time per the published reference.
2. **Warp step.** Before classifying vertex signs, every lattice vertex `v` whose absolute SDF value `|f(v)|` falls below a tabulated `α · ‖e‖` threshold for some incident edge `e` whose other endpoint has opposite SDF sign is *snapped* (vertex displacement) directly to the zero-crossing on `e`. The α tabulation differs for "long" edges (between sublattices) and "short" edges (within a sublattice) per Labelle-Shewchuk Table 1; reference-from-memory placeholders `α_long ≈ 0.21` and `α_short ≈ 0.42` appear elsewhere in this memo as commit-5 starting values, but the exact values are pinned by direct citation from the paper at commit-5 implementation time. Sub-tets sharing a warped vertex inherit the snapped position by construction — no near-vertex sliver can be emitted because the near-vertex case is precisely what warp absorbs.
3. **Stuffing case table.** After warp, classify each BCC tet's 4 vertex signs into a 4-bit case index (16 raw cases). The number of structurally unique cases up to BCC's tet-symmetry group is pinned at commit-5 from the paper's case enumeration; each case emits 0–3 sub-tets with vertex positions at the warped lattice points and (for partial-inside cases) at the zero-crossings on un-warped edges. Edges that warp consumed do not contribute cuts.

**Quality is a theorem.** Labelle-Shewchuk's published quality theorem with their tabulated `α` values guarantees output dihedral angles bounded inside a fixed interval (basic version published as approximately `[10.7°, 164.8°]`; refinements in the paper tighten further) and aspect ratios bounded below a fixed positive constant derived from the dihedral lower bound (≥ `sin(θ_min/2)`-class values, ≈ 0.09 at the basic-version dihedral lower bound). The III-2 sanity floor (`ρ_min ≥ 0.05`, `α_lo ≥ 5°`, `α_hi ≤ 175°`) is cleared with substantial margin under either the basic-version or refined α tabulation — the floor is the *theorem*, not an empirical hope. Any future observed regression below the floor is necessarily a deviation from the published algorithm — surface immediately as a structural bug, not a tuning issue. Exact theorem numbering, exact dihedral interval, and exact α values are pinned at commit-5 from the paper.

**LOC and complexity vs. predecessor.** Pre-pivot estimate was ~300–500 LOC for cubic + MT-clip; BCC + stuffing is ~600–800 LOC (BCC lattice generator ~150, stuffing case table ~250, warp step ~150, vertex de-dup + glue ~100, error paths + scaffolding ~50). Still ~10× smaller than a fTetWild port (~5,000–10,000 LOC for the C++-to-Rust translation of `wildmeshing`). The complexity premium over the pre-pivot placeholder is the warp step + the case table (which is larger than MT's because BCC tets are not regular cubes); both are well-specified in the published paper, and the warp step's correctness is proven, not empirical.

**Why not snap-to-vertex on cubic + MT.** This was Option 1 weighed at the abort signal (Appendix A): bolt a snap-to-vertex tolerance onto MT-clip so cuts within `ε` of a grid corner snap to the corner. Rejected because it papers over the symptom — the placeholder ceiling stays placeholder, no foundation reuse for Phase H, and the tolerance parameter introduces a new tuning knob that the BCC theorem makes unnecessary. BCC + stuffing achieves the same anti-sliver effect through a *proven* mechanism, not a tolerance.

**fTetWild.** Still deferred to Phase H or a later C1 sub-chunk. Its own scope memo + 6-stress-round + grade-A gate. The BCC stage Phase 3 ships is reusable as input to Phase H's eventual envelope-driven (or similar) refinement pass — and the Phase-3 implementation work on case-table reasoning, warp determinism, and bipartite-lattice edge caching translates either way — not a throwaway.

**Decision K (revised post-pivot 2026-04-27): BC system for spatial predicates — `pick_vertices_by_predicate` + `referenced_vertices` free functions.**

Post-pivot, BCC + warp + stuffing produces no orphan vertices by construction (warp displaces in-place; the case table consumes warped vertices without creating coincident duplicates per Decision M's "Pre-pivot orphan-vertex side-effect — no longer applies" note). The orphan-filtering through `referenced_vertices` is preserved in the III-3 test as *defense-in-depth* for general spatial-predicate BCs — the helper is cheap and catches any future case where a vertex set is constructed in a way that produces orphans (e.g., a deliberate post-pass mesh trim, or a downstream mesh-edit operation). **Helpers stay thin** (`pick_vertices_by_predicate`: filter by predicate, return Vec; `referenced_vertices`: collect referenced vertex IDs) rather than overloading either with reduce/argmax variants. **Pin-set sizing rationale:** the bottom-hemisphere predicate is conservative (3 non-collinear pinned vertices suffice to constrain rigid-body modes; hemisphere pins ~half the vertices) but mirrors walking-skeleton's "majority-pin, single-load" pattern (1-tet pinned 3 of 4 vertices) and ensures well-conditioned Hessian for the larger DOF count. **Free functions, not methods on `BoundaryConditions` or `Mesh` trait** — keeps the BC struct schema and Mesh trait surfaces stable; helpers compose across pinned/loaded use cases.

**Decision M (revised 2026-04-27): Determinism guarantees + correctness guarantees — preserve I-5 verbatim, plus four new mitigations.**

**D-8 (determinism, unchanged):** SDF zero-crossing tie-breaking rule — `v ≤ 0` is inside (NOT `v < 0`). Documented in the stuffing module's top-of-file comment. Note: under BCC + warp, edges where this tie-breaker fires *post-warp* are rare (the warp step usually displaces the near-zero vertex first), but the rule still applies for genuinely-on-the-zero-isosurface lattice points that warp leaves alone (a fixed point at exactly zero may stay if the warp threshold is computed against a non-zero edge length).

**D-9 (correctness, reshaped for BCC):** edge-cache key encoding — the BTreeMap key is `((i32, i32, i32, u8), (i32, i32, i32, u8))` — sorted-lexicographic pair `(min, max)` of `(grid_x, grid_y, grid_z, sublattice_tag)` quadruples, where `sublattice_tag ∈ {0, 1}` distinguishes the even sublattice (cube corners) from the odd sublattice (cube centers). Pre-pivot D-9 used `((i32, i32, i32), (i32, i32, i32))` triples because the cubic + Kuhn decomposition only had even-sublattice corners; BCC's bipartite lattice requires the tag to disambiguate `(0,0,0,even) ≠ (0,0,0,odd)` (the latter is at the cube center, physical position offset by `(½, ½, ½) · a`). Edges that warp DOES NOT consume (i.e., partial-inside case cuts) have their cut points cached under this key for O(1) dedup across BCC tet adjacencies. BTreeMap (not HashMap) for sorted-iteration determinism per the same defense-in-depth rationale. III-1's bit-equality across two runs is the explicit detector — any duplicate-vertex bug or sublattice-tag collision would surface as run-to-run vertex-count instability.

**D-10 (correctness, demoted to defensive backstop):** degenerate-tet filter — every output sub-tet is checked against `signed_volume < EPSILON_VOLUME` (default `1e-15 m³`, fixed constant) before being added to the output mesh; sub-tets failing the threshold are silently filtered. Under BCC + Labelle-Shewchuk's published quality theorem the algorithm produces *no* degenerate sub-tets — the warp step is precisely the mechanism that prevents them. D-10 stays in the codebase as a hardened backstop for FP edge cases (e.g., catastrophic cancellation in the warp-distance computation at the limit of f64 precision); under correct operation the filter triggers zero times across the III-2 sweep. III-2's strict `signed_volume > 0` assertion remains the detector — if the filter triggers in production, it indicates either an FP edge case worth investigating or a deviation from the published algorithm. Pre-pivot D-10 was *load-bearing* (handled lattice-on-sphere coincidences in MT-clip on a cubic lattice that the warp step now subsumes); post-pivot it is *insurance*.

**D-11 (new, determinism for the warp step):** the warp pass walks lattice vertices in a fixed `(sublattice_tag, iz, iy, ix)` outer order — even sublattice first, then odd; within each, `z`-major then `y` then `x`. For each vertex, the warp candidates (incident edges) are walked in a fixed neighbor-offset order (long-edge category first per Labelle-Shewchuk's edge classification, then short-edge category; exact neighbor count per category and offset table pinned at commit 5 from the paper's lattice geometry). A vertex is warped to the FIRST zero-crossing it encounters in this order that satisfies the `|f(v)| < α · ‖edge‖` threshold; subsequent candidates are ignored once warped. This rule is deterministic because the iteration order is fixed and the comparison is `f64`-exact. Without this rule, "warp to nearest zero-crossing" would tie-break ambiguously when two candidates have equal `‖edge‖` and equal `|f(v)|` magnitude, leaking determinism. The warp threshold values `α_long` and `α_short` are fixed `const f64` constants pinned at commit-5 by direct citation from the paper (memory-placeholder values `≈0.21` / `≈0.42` used while drafting are NOT the commit values); III-1's bit-equality is the explicit detector for any drift.

**Pre-pivot orphan-vertex side-effect — no longer applies.** Under MT-clip + D-10, lattice-on-sphere coincidences produced duplicate-position orphan vertices that III-3 had to filter via `mesh::referenced_vertices`. Under BCC + warp + stuffing, no orphan vertices are produced: warp displaces near-zero vertices to the zero-crossing in-place (the lattice vertex IS the zero-crossing); the stuffing case table consumes warped vertices as case inputs without creating coincident duplicates. `mesh::referenced_vertices` stays a public helper because it remains useful for general mesh introspection (and is cheap), but Decision K's "load-bearing for III-3" framing relaxes to "defense-in-depth for general spatial-predicate BCs."

## Appendix A — Pre-pivot III-2 evidence and alternatives weighed (2026-04-27)

**Key observations:**

- *Sanity floor failed three of three.* `aspect_min = 0.0001` vs. floor `≥ 0.05` (500× below); `dih_min = 0.16°` vs. floor `≥ 5°` (31× below); `dih_max = 178.22°` vs. floor `≤ 175°` (3.22° over).
- *Scale-invariance confirmed kernel correctness.* Aspect / dihedral extrema repeat exactly across `r` at fixed `cells_per_radius` (e.g., `r=0.05, r=0.10, r=0.20` all show `aspect_min=0.0002` at `r/3`) — `mesh::quality::compute_metrics` is correct; the algorithm is the problem.
- *Filter sized correctly.* `vol_min ≈ 1.9e-14` is one order of magnitude above `EPSILON_VOLUME = 1e-15`; the filter dropped the truly-zero-volume tail; the sliver tail at `vol ~ 1e-14` and above survived.
- *Sliver location.* The pathology is at MT-clip cuts where `t = -v0/(v1-v0)` is near `0` or `1` — the cut lands close to a grid corner without matching it exactly, producing a sub-tet with one near-degenerate edge. Geometric, not parameter-tunable.

### A.2 Alternatives weighed at the abort signal

| Option | Approach | Rejected because |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Snap-to-vertex tolerance on MT-clip | When `t < ε_snap` or `t > 1 − ε_snap`, snap cut to nearer grid corner; eliminates near-vertex slivers by construction. ~30–50 LOC in `marching_tet.rs`. | Papers over the symptom; placeholder ceiling stays placeholder; introduces a tunable parameter the BCC theorem makes unnecessary; no foundation reuse for Phase H (snap-on-MT is thrown away when fTetWild lands). |

| 4. Weaken III-2 to a percentile invariant | E.g., 95% of tets above floor, plus floor on aggregate volume convergence. Keeps placeholder, weakens the gate. | Conflicts with `feedback_fix_gaps_before_continuing`; weakens the regression detector; future regressions slip through; ceiling actively reduced. |

### A.3 Why BCC's ceiling is uniquely high

(Repeated here for the appendix audit trail; canonical statement is in §0.5.)

- *Quality is a theorem, not a hope.* Floor compliance proved, not measured.
- *Same lattice stage fTetWild uses.* Phase H's port becomes additive (envelope-driven refinement on top of an existing BCC lattice), not a replacement.
- *Multi-material aligns naturally.* BCC labels lattice vertices per material and stuffs each region independently with shared warped interfaces.
- *Adaptive refinement maps cleanly.* Octree-refined BCC is the standard foundation for Phase G interactive re-mesh and Phase H's adaptive pass.
- *Well-cited and well-documented.* Labelle-Shewchuk 2007 is one of the most-cited tetrahedral-meshing papers in graphics; the case table fits in ~250 LOC; risk-of-getting-wrong is bounded by the published reference.
